# Gamefowl formula stats-card rubric

## Score scale

- **0** = no plausible support or harmful for this metric
- **1-2** = weak/mostly speculative; major limitation
- **3-4** = plausible mechanism but low confidence, poor timing, or dose/form mismatch
- **5-6** = moderate support; useful but not dominant
- **7-8** = strong support from mechanism + relevant evidence/field data
- **9-10** = central formula strength with direct relevant evidence and dose/form/timing alignment

Confidence:
- **High** = direct avian/poultry evidence or repeated field evidence + chemistry fits
- **Medium** = strong physiology/chemistry, partial avian evidence, or commercial avian precedent
- **Low** = non-avian extrapolation, unclear dose, chronic evidence used for acute claim, or untested shelf-life

## Metric definitions

### Focus / alertness
Score calm sharpness, coordination, and reaction consistency. Do not reward panic, overstimulation, or masking distress.

### Energy availability
Immediate usable fuel and calorie/electrolyte delivery. MCT/oils score only if digestion/emulsion/GI tolerance fit the timing.

### Fatigue/stress tolerance
Ability to maintain movement quality under exertion/heat without hiding injury. Chronic adaptogens/osmolytes score higher when preloaded.

### Fast recovery
Breathing, posture, alertness, water/feed interest, and return to baseline after exertion. Separate visible recovery from molecular mechanisms.

### ATP regeneration
Support for ATP/PCr/nucleotide pool and Mg-ATP function. Do not double-count simple calories as ATP regeneration unless pathway-specific.

### Anti-inflammatory / antioxidant support
Oxidative-stress buffering, tissue-protection, heat-stress support. Wet ascorbate/vitamins can score lower if shelf-life destroys potency.

### Respiratory / acid-base support
Panting recovery, chloride/citrate balance, pulmonary-hypertension/ascites resilience, and avoiding over-alkalinization.

### Hydration / electrolyte balance
Na/K/Cl/Mg/osmolality and voluntary intake. Hypotonic oral solutions should not be over-concentrated.

### GI tolerance
Crop/gut load, droppings, palatability, regurgitation risk, viscosity/syringeability, oil burden, magnesium laxation.

### Onset speed
Rate at which the formula can plausibly help. Score chronic-only ingredients low for same-day acute use unless already preloaded.

### Shelf stability / commercial readiness
6-12 month target requires physical emulsion stability, low browning/oxidation, preservative efficacy at actual pH, pH drift control, packaging, and potency retention.

## Ingredient contribution notation

Use these in the matrix:
- `++` strong positive driver
- `+` modest positive driver
- `0` neutral/not relevant
- `-` negative tradeoff/risk
- `--` serious blocker for that metric
- `?` unknown; testing needed

## Common scoring cautions

- Ribose + primary amines + water + time = browning/Maillard risk; penalize shelf stability.
- DMG is formulation-friendly; do not penalize it like primary amino acids for Maillard.
- L-carnitine is usually more chronic/preload than acute; avoid high acute energy claims.
- Citrate can help buffering but can over-alkalinize if chloride is too low.
- MCT is an oil-phase energy source; in a single bottle, physical stability and GI tolerance may cap the score.
- B vitamins and ascorbate in wet/light/sugar systems may lose potency before use.
