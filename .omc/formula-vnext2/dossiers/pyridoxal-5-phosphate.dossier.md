# Substance Dossier: Pyridoxal-5'-phosphate (P5P / PLP, active vitamin B6)

## 1. Identity
Pyridoxal-5'-phosphate, the active coenzyme form of vitamin B6. Monohydrate: CAS 41468-25-1, MW 265.16. Anhydrous: CAS 54-47-7, MW 247.14. Synonyms: PLP, P5P, pyridoxal phosphate, codecarboxylase. Related B6 forms: pyridoxine HCl (CAS 58-56-0), pyridoxamine, pyridoxamine-5'-phosphate. Pale yellow crystalline powder; light/air/moisture-sensitive (store 2-8 C). In this formula it appears as P5P 10 mg per 450 mg capsule (all variants A/B/C), as "B6 100 mg" in S3 Reset B liquid (per 120 mL), and as liquid B6 within the S4 NUSAVA B-complex. (Tier 2: ChemicalBook CB6757175, Sigma P9255 monographs.)

## 2. Three-rule findings
- **R1 solubility:** PLP monohydrate is "slightly soluble" in water; monograph/supplier figures range ~1 mg/mL (Selleck, MedChemExpress) up to ~5 g/L = 5 mg/mL at 20 C (ChemicalBook CB6757175). Dissolves readily in dilute HCl and in NaOH TS. Intended in-solution concentration in S3 Reset B = 100 mg / 120 mL = 0.83 mg/mL; the 10 mg capsule dose is dry so solubility is moot there. Headroom vs the conservative ~1 mg/mL figure = ~1.2 (TIGHT); vs the 5 mg/mL figure = ~6 (OK). Net: solubility is TIGHT-to-OK as a number but is NOT the binding constraint here — stability is (see R2). In the winning dry-preload design solubility is irrelevant. Verdict on solubility alone: OK-but-irrelevant for dry; TIGHT if forced into S3 liquid. (Tier 2: ChemicalBook CB6757175; Selleck; MedChemExpress. Tier 1: J. Chem. Eng. Data 2018, DOI 10.1021/acs.jced.8b00309, solubility correlation in binary solvents.)
- **R2 stability:** PLP is highly photolabile and pH-sensitive in aqueous solution. Stable in acid; degrades in neutral/alkaline solution and under light. Measured photolysis half-life ~8 h in light vs ~720 h kept dark — a ~90-fold loss driven solely by light exposure (Mohamed-Ahmed et al., extemporaneous PLP stability study, PMID 28220480). Key irreversible photodegradation product is 4-pyridoxic acid 5'-phosphate (PAP); degradation is O2- and concentration-dependent. Separately, PLP undergoes hydrolytic DEPHOSPHORYLATION to free pyridoxal; soluble/erythrocyte phosphatase activity has a pH optimum of ~6.0-6.5 — which OVERLAPS the formula pH 5.8-6.2 exactly, so the phosphate ester sits in its least-protected pH window in this matrix (Gamov 2022, Int J Chem Kinetics, DOI 10.1002/kin.21541; classic phosphatase optimum pH 6-6.5). Realistic aqueous shelf-life of an unbuffered, lit, near-neutral PLP solution is hours-to-days, not months. Storage: 2-8 C, protect from light, air and moisture. Verdict: BLOCK in any wet near-neutral SKU; dry capsule/feed premix is the correct home. (Tier 1: PMID 28220480. Tier 2: DOI 10.1002/kin.21541.)
- **R3 conflicts:** (1) Aldehyde Schiff-base / Maillard reactivity — PLP's free C-4' aldehyde forms nonspecific Schiff-base adducts with amines and thiols (Front Mol Biosci 2019, DOI 10.3389/fmolb.2019.00004). The formula is loaded with free amino groups (L-glutamine, L-tyrosine, L-phenylalanine, L-arginine, citrulline, taurine, betaine) and a thiol (NAC), plus reducing sugars in S2/S5 (corn syrup, maltodextrin, D-ribose). Pair -> mechanism -> mitigation: PLP + free amino acid / NAC thiol -> Schiff base / adduct that consumes the active aldehyde and the partner active -> keep PLP DRY, away from amino acids and NAC in solution. PLP + reducing sugar + heat -> browning/Maillard -> never co-formulate PLP in the S2 sugar matrix. (2) Phosphate ester + divalent metals (Mg in Mg-glycerophosphate / Mg-malate / MgCl2) -> potential Mg-phosphate interaction/precipitation at the phosphate moiety -> dry separation avoids it (Mg lives in feed, not the drink). (3) pH conflict: ester dephosphorylation optimum pH 6-6.5 overlaps the formula pH -> either strongly acidify or keep dry. Overall mitigation: consolidate ALL B6 to a single dry source in the preload; remove from every liquid.

## 3. Avian/poultry evidence
Poultry B6 requirement ~2.5-4.5 mg/kg diet (NRC Nutrient Requirements of Poultry 1994; Merck Vet Manual). Deficiency signs: retarded growth, dermatitis, anemia, reduced nitrogen retention (B6 central to protein/amino-acid metabolism). Maximum tolerable: poultry-specific data are insufficient; NRC extrapolates (from rat/dog) that ~50x requirement (~125-225 mg/kg feed) is safe for most species, with overt neurotoxicity (ataxia, incoordination, muscle weakness) appearing only near ~1000x requirement (NRC Vitamin Tolerance of Animals, ch.9 Vitamin B6). Acute vs chronic: B6 is water-soluble and renally cleared in uricotelic birds, so moderate single-dose excess is excreted rather than acutely accumulated; the real risk is chronic/daily accumulation toward neurotoxic multiples. Dose range: 3-5 mg/bird/day dry keeps a 2.5 kg bird within requirement-to-modest-excess and meets the <15 mg/session formula cap by construction when B6 is a single source. (Tier 1: NRC Poultry 1994; NRC Vitamin Tolerance of Animals; Merck Vet Manual.)

## 4. Verdict
ADD-conditional, DRY ONLY — place in the dry PRELOAD feed premix as a single consolidated B6 source delivering ~3-5 mg/bird/day (dry P5P, or the more shelf-stable pyridoxine-HCl). SKIP / BLOCK in every wet near-neutral SKU: remove the S3 Reset B "B6 100 mg / 120 mL" entirely and drop B6 from the S4 B-complex liquid. Rationale: dry placement eliminates the photolysis (8h vs 720h), the pH 6-6.5 dephosphorylation overlap, and the Schiff-base/Maillard adduct losses simultaneously, and keeps session B6 well under 15 mg.

## 5. Cautions
(a) Sum B6 across ALL sources: legacy S3 100 mg + S4 B-complex + 10 mg capsule could stack toward chronic neurotoxic multiples on daily use — the single-dry-source rule is the fix. (b) Daily (vs occasional event) dosing raises chronic-accumulation concern far more than acute use. (c) Dry P5P is hygroscopic and air-sensitive — foil-pack with desiccant, light-protected even when dry. (d) Pyridoxine-HCl is a more shelf-stable dry substitute and is standard/adequate for poultry B6 repletion; birds phosphorylate pyridoxine endogenously, so the active P5P form is not specifically required. (e) Welfare-positive within scope: corrects/prevents B6 deficiency and supports amino-acid metabolism during exertion.

Corrections: R2 MISATTRIBUTION (load-bearing): The specific '8h light / 720h dark half-life' figures are NOT from PMID 28220480. The exact 8h vs 720h half-lives come from a different paper, the visible-light PLP-dependent enzyme study (PMC8251830, ChemCatChem/related). Fix: keep PMID 28220480 for the photolability/PAP/24h-dark-4h-light claims, but re-cite the 8h-vs-720h number to PMC8251830 (or drop the precise number and state 'hours in light vs weeks in dark').; R2 OVERSTATED pH claim (load-bearing weak point): The 'phosphatase dephosphorylation pH optimum 6.0-6.5 overlaps formula pH 5.8-6.2 exactly = least-protected window' claim is not supported by its cited DOI. There is no microbial/enzymatic phosphatase present in this preservative-protected drink anyway, so enzymatic dephosphorylation in-bottle is largely moot; the real in-solution risk is non-enzymatic photolysis + Schiff-base loss.
