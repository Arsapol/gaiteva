# Substance Dossier: L-Phenylalanine

## 1. Identity
L-Phenylalanine (LAF; "Phe"; "F"). IUPAC: (S)-2-amino-3-phenylpropanoic acid; synonyms: (S)-alpha-amino-beta-phenylpropionic acid, L-beta-phenylalanine, H-Phe-OH. Formula C9H11NO2, MW 165.19 g/mol. CAS 63-91-2. Essential aromatic amino acid; supplied as the free base (zwitterion) in pharma/food grade (USP/Ph.Eur./FCC monographs exist). pI ~5.48; pKa1 (alpha-COOH) ~1.83-2.16, pKa2 (alpha-NH3+) ~9.13-9.24 [Tier 2, Chegg/Vaia textbook pKa set; isoelectric pI=(pKa1+pKa2)/2]. In this formula L-Phenylalanine appears ONLY in the dry capsules (variants A/B/C = 10/60/30 mg per 450 mg cap), paired with L-Tyrosine as a catecholamine (dopamine/noradrenaline) precursor stack in the S1 FOCUS capsule. It is NOT present in any liquid SKU (S2-S5, F5).

## 2. Three-rule findings
- **R1 solubility:** Aqueous solubility of L-phenylalanine in pure water at 25C (298.15 K) ~27-28 g/L (measured 0.1675 mol/kg x 165.19 g/mol = ~27.7 g/L) [Tier 1, J. Chem. Eng. Japan 43(9):2010, "Solubility of L-Phenylalanine in Aqueous Solutions", DOI 10.1252/jcej.10we013; corroborated ScienceDirect Fluid Phase Equilibria 2011 DOI 10.1016/j.fluid.2011.10.004 for 288-318 K]. Solubility rises with temperature. INTENDED USE IS DRY (capsule), so solubility headroom is not gating for the current formula. HYPOTHETICAL in-solution check (if ever dissolved): the catecholamine-precursor stack would target on the order of L-Tyrosine ~50-110 mg + L-Phe ~10-60 mg per dose; even a full FOCUS dose of ~60 mg Phe in a 1-2 mL concentrate (~30-60 g/L) would meet or exceed Phe's ~28 g/L ceiling and is co-limited far more severely by L-Tyrosine (sol only ~0.45 g/L). Verdict for Phe alone as a dilute drink additive (<=~1 g/L target): headroom >27 = OK. As a high-concentration concentrate co-mixed with tyrosine: TIGHT-to-BLOCK, but driven by tyrosine, not Phe. Current dry-capsule route: solubility moot/OK.
- **R2 stability:** Free L-phenylalanine is chemically robust: a simple, non-phosphorylated, non-thiol, non-easily-oxidized aromatic amino acid. No facile hydrolysis (no labile ester/amide/glycosidic bond — the carboxyl/amine are the free zwitterion), and it is not redox-labile like ascorbate, NAC-thiol, P5P, or B12. The benzyl ring is not appreciably photolabile at visible/near-UV. Dry capsule storage: very stable, multi-year shelf-life with normal desiccated packaging (USP/FCC commodity amino acid). KEY in-solution caveat = MAILLARD/STRECKER: as a primary alpha-amino acid, Phe reacts with reducing sugars (glucose/dextrose, ribose, maltodextrin reducing ends) on heating/standing to give Strecker aldehyde phenylacetaldehyde + browning pigments [Tier 1/2, Food Sci. Technol. Res. 9(1):1-6 review of Strecker degradation; J. Agric. Food Chem. DOI 10.1021/jf048883r phenylalanine -> phenylacetaldehyde]. This is why Phe must NOT be co-formulated wet with the S2 reducing-sugar matrix (corn syrup + maltodextrin + D-ribose + heat) — it would add to the existing Maillard load and generate off-odor/browning. Stable pH window: as zwitterion it is soluble and stable across the formula pH ~5.8-6.2; no pH-driven degradation in that band. Net: dry = highly stable; wet-with-sugars = Maillard-reactive. Preservative interaction: Phe does not undermine K-sorbate, but the sorbate-at-pH-6 efficacy problem is a separate (liquid-architecture) issue Phe never touches because it stays dry.
- **R3 conflicts:** Phe is dry-capsule-only in this formula, so it shares no solution with any liquid ingredient and has NO wet-phase pairwise conflict in the current design. Potential conflicts IF moved to a liquid: (1) Reducing sugars (S2 dextrose/corn syrup/maltodextrin/D-ribose) -> Maillard/Strecker browning (mechanism above; mitigation: keep dry, never co-pack with sugar matrix). (2) Co-formulation with L-Tyrosine in a concentrate -> shared low-solubility ceiling dominated by tyrosine (~0.45 g/L) causing precipitation; mitigation: keep both in dry capsule (current design). (3) Physiological/transport competition: Phe, Tyr, and Tryptophan are Large Neutral Amino Acids (LNAA) sharing the LAT1/L-system transporter; high doses of one competitively reduce uptake of the others (and at the blood-brain barrier) — relevant because the FOCUS stack co-d:oses Phe+Tyr; mechanism is competitive, not chemical; mitigation: keep ratio modest, which the formula does (Tyr >= Phe in all variants). No chelation/redox conflict (Phe is not a metal chelator of concern and not redox-active), so it does NOT aggravate the Fe/Cu-catalyzed ascorbate/NAC oxidation seen elsewhere. No acid-base/DEB contribution of note (neutral amino acid, near pI in feed; negligible mEq impact).

## 3. Avian/poultry evidence
Phenylalanine is a DIETARY-ESSENTIAL aromatic amino acid for chickens; it is the metabolic precursor of tyrosine via hepatic phenylalanine hydroxylase, so Phe deficiency also lowers Tyr [Tier 1, J. Anim. Sci. Biotechnol. 2021 DOI 10.1186/s40104-021-00568-0; Poult. Sci. broiler-breeder maintenance study PMID 26994191]. REQUIREMENT (combined Phe+Tyr, since they are interconvertible): broiler 14-28 d total Phe+Tyr ~1.09-1.12% of diet (~6.1-6.2% of dietary protein), of which ~55% is supplied as Phe in adequate diets [Tier 1, jasbsci review 2021; PMC5444564 "optimal ratios of digestible Phe+Tyr...to Lys in broilers"]. Digestible Phe+Tyr:Lys ideal ratio ~ near 1.0-1.12 depending on endpoint. MAX TOLERABLE / TOXICITY: free L-Phe is well tolerated; classic excess-amino-acid toxicity in poultry centers on Phe/Tyr imbalance and very high single-AA loads causing growth depression, not on small mg-scale supplemental doses. No avian acute-vs-chronic ergogenic/recovery trial supports an ACUTE catecholamine-precursor "focus/drive" effect in birds — the per-capsule 10-60 mg dose for a 2.5 kg bird is nutritionally trivial vs the gram-scale daily Phe+Tyr requirement (a 2.5 kg bird eating ~120-150 g feed at ~1.1% Phe+Tyr already gets >1 g/day Phe+Tyr). MECHANISM (catecholamine synthesis Phe->Tyr->L-DOPA->dopamine->noradrenaline) is real biochemistry but there is NO peer-reviewed avian evidence that supplemental Phe acutely raises CNS catecholamine drive or improves performance/recovery in chickens. CLASSIFY: nutritionally essential (dietary), but as an ACUTE ergogenic capsule additive = NO AVIAN PERFORMANCE DATA (Tier 3 rationale only).

## 4. Verdict
SKIP as a deliberate acute ergogenic/"focus" additive; if any Phe is retained, keep it DRY in the preload/capsule only. Verdict per phase: (a) Fight-day drink / any liquid -> SKIP/BLOCK addition (Maillard-reactive with the sugar-based ORS; no acute avian benefit; adds nothing to SGLT1 rehydration). (b) Dry capsule (current S1 FOCUS, 10-60 mg) -> ADD-conditional at best, but LOW VALUE: the 10-60 mg/dose is nutritionally negligible against the bird's >1 g/day dietary Phe+Tyr supply, and there is NO avian evidence for an acute catecholamine "drive" effect, so it is effectively inert window-dressing. Recommendation: DROP from the acute stack (mirrors the design's demotion of non-evidenced actives like D-ribose/Rhodiola); if a methyl/aromatic-AA program is ever wanted it belongs in the chronic dry PRELOAD as part of balanced dietary protein, not as a per-round capsule. Dose if kept (dry, chronic only): treat as part of dietary Phe+Tyr to the ~1.1% diet requirement, not as a bolus. Net for vNext two-SKU design: NOT carried into either the dry preload as a standalone or the fight-day drink.

## 5. Cautions
Welfare/safety notes (non-blocking): (1) Free L-Phe is generally safe in birds at supplemental mg doses; main theoretical risk is amino-acid antagonism/imbalance (Phe-Tyr-Trp LNAA competition) if dosed disproportionately, which the current Tyr>=Phe ratio avoids. (2) Maillard browning/off-flavor if ever dissolved in the reducing-sugar liquids — keep dry. (3) Phenylketonuria (PKU) is a HUMAN caution (handlers should avoid ingesting the powder if PKU); not relevant to the bird. (4) Evidence honesty: the "focus/catecholamine precursor" rationale is human-supplement extrapolation with NO avian performance/recovery trial — flag as marginal ROI, same tier as the other demoted non-evidenced actives. (5) Repeated daily high-dose aromatic AA loading is not advised; occasional event-use only if retained at all.
