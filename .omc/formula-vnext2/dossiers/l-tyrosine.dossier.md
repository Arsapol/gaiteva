# Substance Dossier: L-Tyrosine

## 1. Identity
L-Tyrosine (4-hydroxy-L-phenylalanine). Free amino acid, white crystalline powder. CAS 60-18-4. MW 181.19 g/mol (Wikipedia/PubChem CID 6057). Synonyms: (S)-2-amino-3-(4-hydroxyphenyl)propanoic acid, (S)-alpha-amino-4-hydroxybenzenepropanoic acid, Tyr, Y. pKa: alpha-COOH ~2.2, alpha-NH3+ ~9.1, phenolic-OH ~10.1; isoelectric point pI ~5.6 (brainly/standard biochem). Salt forms exist (L-tyrosine disodium salt hydrate, CAS 69847-45-6) for solubility but NOT used in this formula. In CURRENT formula appears ONLY in dry CAPSULES (S1 variants): 110 mg (A) / 50 mg (B) / 60 mg (C) per 450 mg cap. NOT present in any liquid (S2-S5, F5).

## 2. Three-rule findings
- **R1 solubility:** Aqueous solubility ~0.45 mg/mL (0.453 g/L = 45.3 mg/100 mL) at 25C in water at pH ~3.5-7.5 [Merck Index; Wikipedia Tyrosine, both Tier 2]. This is one of the LEAST water-soluble proteinogenic amino acids. Solubility is MINIMUM at the isoelectric point (pI ~5.6) and rises only at extreme pH (<~2 or >~10) [Evonik cell-culture technical note, Tier 2; pI from standard biochem, Tier 2]. CRITICAL FOR THIS FORMULA: the liquid SKUs and the proposed fight-day drink sit at pH ~5.8-6.2, i.e. essentially ON the pI = the solubility floor. INTENDED USE: tyrosine is only in DRY CAPSULES, so as-formulated there is NO in-solution concentration and R1 is not triggered (dry = N/A, OK). HYPOTHETICAL if put in the drink: even a token 0.5 g/L acute-CNS dose >> 0.45 g/L saturation -> headroom <1 -> BLOCK (would precipitate/never dissolve at pH 6). To reach a meaningful ~100 mg/bird in the ~96 mL effective intake would require ~1 g/L, ~2x over saturation. Verdict R1: dry capsule = OK; any attempt to solubilize in the pH 5.8-6.2 liquid = BLOCK (headroom <<1).
- **R2 stability:** Solid L-tyrosine is highly stable: thermal decomposition onset ~340C, main pathway decarboxylation (TG-FTIR, Tier 2); racemization only under prolonged sunlight/UV/high heat (ScienceDirect LDH-intercalation study, Tier 2). In aqueous solution the free amino acid backbone is hydrolytically stable (no labile ester/amide); not a notable oxidizer or photolytic loss species at supplement timescales (contrast B12/ascorbate/P5P/NAC). The phenolic ring is in principle a mild reducing/Maillard-participating amine+phenol, but the dominant limiter is solubility, not degradation. As a DRY CAPSULE active there is no water, no hydrolysis, no Maillard, no preservative exposure -> stability is a non-issue (the same dry-first logic the redesign applies to B12/P5P/NAC). Formula pH ~5.8-6.2 vs stable window: tyrosine itself is chemically stable across this range; the problem at this pH is purely solubility (pI). No preservative concern because it is dry. Realistic shelf-life in a desiccated capsule: years. Verdict R2: STABLE; storage = dry, cool, in capsule/sachet (no special light/O2 precaution beyond good practice). No degradation BLOCK.
- **R3 conflicts:** As formulated (dry capsule, co-located with Creatine, L-Glutamine, Mg-glycerophosphate, NAC, L-Phenylalanine, Rhodiola, P5P, +/- L-Arginine), no wet-phase interactions occur. Pairwise notes:
- Tyrosine <-> L-Phenylalanine (same cap, 10-60 mg): Phe converts to Tyr in birds (phenylalanine hydroxylase); they share the large-neutral-amino-acid (LNAA/LAT1) transporter, so high co-dosed LNAAs (Phe, and any added BCAA) competitively blunt brain tyrosine uptake -> physiological antagonism, not a chemical one. Mitigation: keep Phe+Tyr ratio reasonable; the CNS rationale for tyrosine is weakened by co-dosed Phe. [Phe->Tyr conversion: ScienceDirect "Phe and Tyr requirements young chick", Tier 1.]
- Tyrosine <-> P5P (same cap): P5P/B6 is the cofactor for aromatic-amino-acid decarboxylase and tyrosine aminotransferase; co-presence is synergistic, not conflicting.
- Tyrosine <-> divalent metals (Mg-glycerophosphate in cap; Ca/Mg/citrate in liquids): the phenolic-OH can weakly chelate transition metals, but at dry-capsule scale and with Mg (not redox-active Fe/Cu) this is negligible; no precipitation pathway in the dry state.
- HYPOTHETICAL drink conflict: if anyone solubilized tyrosine into a citrate/electrolyte drink, the dominant failure is precipitation at pH ~6 (pI), and divalent cations would not rescue it. -> reinforce: keep tyrosine OUT of any liquid.
Verdict R3: no real conflict as dry capsule; only a soft physiological LNAA-transport competition with co-dosed Phe/BCAAs to note. Mitigation = keep tyrosine dry; do not move it into the drink.

## 3. Avian/poultry evidence
Tyrosine is a (conditionally) dietary aromatic amino acid in chickens; it is non-essential ONLY insofar as it can be made from essential phenylalanine, so Phe+Tyr are pooled as the "total aromatic amino acid" requirement. POULTRY REQUIREMENT: young-chick max-growth requirement Tyr ~0.37% +/- 0.05% of diet and Phe ~0.50% +/- 0.07%, with Tyr supplying up to ~42.5% of the aromatic-AA total [Phe/Tyr requirements young chick, ScienceDirect S0032579119474749, Tier 1]. Combined Phe+Tyr requirement ~1.09-1.12% of diet (~6.1-6.2% of dietary protein) for 14-28 d broilers [PMC5444564 Phe+Tyr:Lys ratio, Tier 1; broiler-breeder maintenance Phe+Tyr/Tyr requirements PMID 26994191, Tier 1]. HEAT-STRESS / STRESS DATA: chronic heat stress raises hepatic tyrosine aminotransferase activity, and the enzyme response scales with dietary Tyr (50/100/200% NRC); heat stress cut weight gain and feed intake at all Tyr levels — i.e. extra dietary tyrosine did NOT rescue heat-stress performance [PMID 12365521, Tier 1]. Heat stress also activates Phe/Tyr biosynthesis and mobilizes catecholamines (Tyr is the catecholamine precursor) [neuroendocrine heat-stress review PMC11591812, Tier 2]. ACUTE CNS/PERFORMANCE: the human acute-tyrosine-in-heat ergogenic rationale is WEAK even in humans (150 mg/kg gave no cognitive or performance benefit, PMC4580763, Tier 1) and there is NO avian study showing an ACUTE oral tyrosine bolus improves fight-day/round-to-round performance. NO AVIAN ACUTE-DOSING EFFICACY DATA for tyrosine as an inter-round supplement. Max tolerable: not a freely-excreted electrolyte; as an aromatic AA it is metabolized, not simply renally dumped, so "uricotelic excess is harmless" does NOT fully apply — excess aromatic AA can cause amino-acid imbalance/antagonism. No specific poultry toxic ceiling located -> UNKNOWN upper limit; doses near requirement are safe, gram-scale acute boluses are unstudied in birds.

## 4. Verdict
SKIP for any liquid / fight-day drink (R1 BLOCK in water at pH 5.8-6.2: solubility ~0.45 g/L sits at the pI floor; a meaningful ~1 g/L dose is ~2x over saturation and will not dissolve). KEEP-AS-DRY-CAPSULE only, but ADD-conditional/low-priority: tyrosine's value is chronic aromatic-AA adequacy and catecholamine-precursor support under heat stress, NOT an acute inter-round lever — and even the chronic heat-stress benefit is unproven (PMID 12365521 showed no performance rescue). RECOMMENDATION: (a) NEVER put L-tyrosine in the drink or any liquid — it is one of the least soluble amino acids and the formula pH is its solubility minimum; if a CNS/catecholamine rationale is wanted it must stay dry (capsule or dry preload top-dress). (b) If retained, treat as a modest dry dose ensuring Phe+Tyr meets ~1.1% diet aromatic-AA requirement (chronic), roughly the existing 50-110 mg/cap range is fine; do not escalate to gram-scale chasing an acute effect that has no avian evidence. (c) Lower priority than creatine/betaine/NAC/electrolytes. Phase: dry capsule or dry PRELOAD only. Dose: keep <=~100-150 mg/bird/day dry; do not solubilize.

## 5. Cautions
WELFARE/SAFETY (non-blocking notes): (1) No avian evidence that an ACUTE oral tyrosine bolus improves round-to-round performance; the acute-CNS rationale is imported from weak human data (PMC4580763 null) — do not over-promise. (2) Tyrosine is the catecholamine (epinephrine/norepinephrine/dopamine) precursor; under fight + heat stress the sympatho-adrenal axis is already maximally mobilized — adding precursor has no demonstrated benefit and is theoretically arousal-increasing; this is a welfare consideration for a stress-loaded bird, recorded as a note, not a chemistry blocker. (3) Competes with phenylalanine and other large neutral amino acids on the LAT1 transporter, so co-dosing in the same capsule partially self-cancels CNS delivery. (4) Excess aromatic AA is metabolized (not freely renally dumped like electrolytes), so the uricotelic "excess-is-harmless" frame does NOT fully cover gram-scale tyrosine — keep near requirement. (5) PRODUCIBILITY: extremely low solubility makes it a hard fail in any drink; if a formulator tries the disodium salt to force it into solution, that adds Na load and alkali and is not recommended for the near-neutral Cl-balanced drink target. Keep dry.

## 9. Blocking gaps / UNKNOWN
- No avian study of an ACUTE oral L-tyrosine bolus on fight-day/inter-round performance — acute ergogenic claim is UNSUPPORTED in birds (human data null).
- No located poultry upper tolerable / toxicity ceiling for supplemental L-tyrosine above requirement — max-tolerable is UNKNOWN; safety asserted only near the ~0.37-1.1% aromatic-AA requirement range.
- Solubility values are Tier 2 (Merck Index/Wikipedia/Evonik); a Tier-1 pharmacopeial intrinsic-solubility-vs-pH curve was not directly retrieved (USP monograph confirms identity but not a numeric solubility-pH table) — the ~0.45 g/L floor at pH 6 is consistently corroborated but flagged as Tier 2.
