# Substance Dossier: L-Citrulline / Citrulline malate

## 1. Identity
L-Citrulline (2-amino-5-ureidopentanoic acid; (S)-2-amino-5-(carbamoylamino)pentanoic acid), non-proteinogenic alpha-amino acid. CAS 372-75-8; MW 175.19. Synonyms: L-Cit, citrulline. Salt/cocrystal form: Citrulline malate = L-citrulline + DL-malic acid (malic acid CAS 6915-15-7), commonly 2:1 citrulline:malate molar. For this formula the recommended form is FREE L-citrulline (NOT the malate) to avoid added malate organic-acid/alkali load.

## 2. Three-rule findings
- **R1 solubility:** Aqueous solubility ~200 g/L at 20C; temperature-dependent 102 -> 210 g per 1000 g water across 20-40C (293.15-313.15 K) [DOI 10.1021/acs.jced.5b00585, Tier 1; ScienceDirect S0963996925022136, Tier 2]. Intended fight-day drink concentration 1.0-1.3 g/L. Headroom = ~200/1.3 ~= 150-200x, far above the >=3 OK threshold => OK. Citrulline malate is even more soluble. Solubility is a non-issue at this concentration; in the dry-stick design solubility is moot for storage and trivial on reconstitution. No pharmacopeial 25C monograph value located (have 20C and 20-40C range from primary chem-eng literature, not USP/EP).
- **R2 stability:** Citrulline is a stable amino acid in solution near formula pH. Zwitterion: carboxyl pKa ~2.5, side-chain pKb ~11.5 [chemicalbook CAS 372-75-8, Tier 2], so near-neutral net charge at pH 5.8-6.2 with minimal buffering/alkali contribution. Degradation: the ureido side chain is hydrolytically labile only under strong alkali (1 N NaOH, 72 h causes hydrolysis) [USPTO US8178721B2, Tier 1]; the standard stabilization target is neutralization to pH 5.9 +/- 0.2, which the formula pH 5.8-6.2 matches squarely => stable window. No significant photolysis or oxidation. Thermal decomposition onset ~486 K (213C) [ScienceDirect S0963996925022136, Tier 2]. Maillard: citrulline has a FREE alpha-amino group and CAN participate in non-enzymatic browning with reducing sugars (dextrose) plus heat over time. A dry stick reconstituted fresh and discarded same-day avoids hydrolysis, Maillard, and microbial growth. Realistic: dry stick 12-18 mo; reconstituted drink make-fresh, single fight-day only.
- **R3 conflicts:** Pair -> mechanism -> mitigation: (1) L-citrulline + dextrose (fight-day stick): free alpha-amino group + glucose + heat -> Maillard browning over time -> mitigate by dry stick, reconstitute fresh, discard after session [Maillard chemistry, Tier 2]. (2) L-citrulline + divalent metals (Ca/Mg/Zn): amino acid, weak chelator, no precipitation expected; Mg is moved to feed so no drink interaction. (3) Citrulline MALATE form: malate anion metabolizes to bicarbonate -> mild alkalinizing/DEB lift, counter to the near-neutral Cl-balanced target -> mitigate by using FREE L-citrulline, not malate. (4) L-citrulline + L-arginine: physiologically ADDITIVE for the NO/arginine axis, not antagonistic. No redox conflict with NAC/ascorbate (citrulline is not a thiol or strong reductant).

## 3. Avian/poultry evidence
STRONG avian data. Arginine is STRICTLY essential in birds (no urea cycle); citrulline->arginine sparing is valid. Requirement framing: chronic dietary doses studied 0.25-1.0% diet (~2.5-10 g/kg feed); no avian neurotoxic/max-tolerable ceiling identified (freely metabolized amino acid). PMID 36721201 (Uyanga 2023, J Anim Sci Biotechnol 14:9): 0.50% dietary L-citrulline in arginine-deficient broilers raised plasma arginine/ornithine above the deficient diet and plasma citrulline above direct arginine, no growth penalty -> citrulline arginine-sparing in a urea-cycle-free model. Heat-stress/thermotolerance (CHRONIC): Poultry Sci 2022 DOI 10.1016/j.psj.2021.101669 (central+peripheral hypothermic effect, lowers core temp); J Thermal Biol 2022 DOI 10.1016/j.jtherbio.2022.103227 (1% L-Cit improved immunoglobulin, mitochondrial TFAM/ATP5beta, NO synthesis under heat); PMID 36003654 (Front Physiol 2022, growth/AA profile/GH-IGF axis high temp); PMID 33062264 (laying hens summer: up SOD, up CAT, down MDA). Acute vs chronic: robust avian evidence is CHRONIC dietary feeding; the acute gram-scale drink dose is the one defensible acute lever (NO/arginine pool/vasodilation) but is extrapolated from mammalian gram-scale plus avian chronic mechanism -- NO avian acute single-session dose-response published.

## 4. Verdict
ADD-conditional. FIGHT-DAY DRINK: free L-citrulline 1.0-1.3 g/L (~96-125 mg/session for a 2.5 kg bird) as the single gram-scale acute lever (arginine-sparing NO axis). Use FREE L-citrulline, NOT citrulline-malate, to avoid malate alkali load and preserve the near-neutral Cl-balanced DEB target. ALSO valid as CHRONIC PRELOAD in feed at 0.25-1% (~2.5-10 g/kg) for heat-stress/thermotolerance/antioxidant support per avian PMIDs. SKIP the malate form for the drink. Keep out of any reducing-sugar liquid stored wet (Maillard).

## 5. Cautions
(1) Make-fresh discipline: citrulline + dextrose solution can brown (Maillard) and grow microbes if stored warm; use single-serve dry stick, discard after session. (2) Acute ergogenic evidence in mammals is mixed/equivocal (citrulline malate RCTs, PMC8571142); avian acute single-session performance benefit is mechanistically plausible (NO/arginine) but the robust avian data are CHRONIC heat-stress, not acute. (3) Citrulline is hypothermic in broilers (lowers core temperature) -- generally welfare-positive under heat, but note the thermal effect. (4) Overall welfare-positive: supports the bird's own hydration/NO/antioxidant physiology. Non-blocking notes only.
