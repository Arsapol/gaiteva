## Welfare / Safety / Producibility Cautions (written notes — non-blocking)

**Welfare (scope = the bird's own recovery/hydration; dual-use, welfare-positive):**
- The SGLT1 hypotonic ORS + chronic antioxidant/osmolyte preload are welfare-positive: better rehydration, heat-stress and oxidative protection, ascites/pulmonary-hypertension mitigation (NAC, DMG, carnitine, betaine).
- Main residual welfare risk is microbial safety of any WET product — the dry/make-fresh design specifically removes it.

**Make-fresh discipline (the one operational risk that replaces the chemistry BLOCK):**
- The fight-day drink MUST be reconstituted fresh and discarded same-day. If a handler stores reconstituted sugar/citrulline solution warm for hours it can grow microbes. Mitigation: single-serve sticks, on-label "discard after session," acidified to pH ~3.8–4.2.

**Dosing caps (all met by construction):**
- B6 is dry-only single source → 0 mg/session in drink, ~4 mg/bird/day chronic — well under <15 mg/session. If any legacy B6-containing liquid is re-introduced by a handler, the cap can be breached; legacy liquids are DELETED and the label states single source.
- Mg is feed-only → 0 mg/session in drink. If feed Mg climbs toward 0.3–0.5% the enteric/laxative (wet-litter) ceiling applies — monitor droppings on scale-up.
- Repeated DAILY (vs occasional event) use shifts Na-load and B6-accumulation risk; the acute-safe conclusion is for OCCASIONAL event use.

**Timing / producibility:**
- Preload needs ~7–10 d lead time and assumes feed intake; a bird off-feed pre-event gets no creatine/betaine loading (timing risk, not chemistry).
- Hygroscopic actives (betaine, DMG/Na-DMG, trehalose dihydrate, Mg-glycerophosphate) and oxidation-prone NAC require foil + desiccant packaging; add NAC last under low humidity; geometric 2-step dilution for the B6/B12 micro-premix for uniformity.
- Trehalose dihydrate can cake/crystallize and shifts blend moisture — keep desiccated. (C. difficile dietary-trehalose virulence signal, PMID 29310122, is of UNKNOWN avian relevance — non-blocking monitoring note.)
- Cyanocobalamin substituted for methyl-B12 is a deliberate stability trade (more shelf-stable, adequate for poultry status repletion); confirm the methyl form is not specifically required.
- If a WET bottled SKU is ever forced against advice: pH ≤5.0–5.2 + a validated challenge-tested preservative system (NOT sorbate alone), never NAC/B12/P5P in it. Dry is strictly superior on producibility, shelf-life, and safety.
