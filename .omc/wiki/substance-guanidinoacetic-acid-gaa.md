# Substance: Guanidinoacetic Acid (GAA)

# Substance Dossier: Guanidinoacetic Acid (GAA)

## 1. Identity
Guanidinoacetic acid (N-amidinoglycine). Creatine biosynthetic precursor (GAA + SAM-methyl → creatine). Commercial feed grade = Creamino. CAS 352-97-6.

## 2. Three-rule findings
- **R1 solubility:** ~3.6 g/L water (1 g / 278 mL, ~15°C) — LOW. CORRECTED from earlier 2.5 g/L (verifier). For a drink this is R1-TIGHT/BLOCK at gram doses → use as DRY feed additive (preload), where R1 does not apply.
- **R2 stability:** stable as dry feed additive; good pelleting/thermal stability (a key advantage over creatine).
- **R3 conflicts:** raises methyl-donor (SAM) demand → pair with betaine/choline. No precipitation issues in dry feed.

## 3. Avian/poultry evidence
Broilers fed GAA 0.6–1.2 g/kg feed → ↑ breast-muscle creatine & PCr, improved energy status, BW gain, FCR, breast yield (Michiels 2012 PMID 22252354, VERIFIED). Arginine-sparing (spares dietary arginine for creatine synthesis). Loading is CHRONIC (weeks). >1.5 g/kg not advised.

## 4. Verdict
ADD-strong — PRELOAD phase only (chronic PCr loading). Preferred over direct creatine for STABILITY + COST (~40% cheaper), NOT solubility. NOT a fight-day acute fix.
