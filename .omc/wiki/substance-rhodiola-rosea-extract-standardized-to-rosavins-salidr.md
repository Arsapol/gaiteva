# Substance: Rhodiola rosea extract (standardized to rosavins + salidroside)

# Substance Dossier: Rhodiola rosea extract (standardized to rosavins + salidroside)

## 1. Identity
Rhodiola rosea L. (golden root / arctic root) root/rhizome extract, typically standardized to 3% rosavins + 1% salidroside (e.g., SHR-5 / WS1375 clinical extracts). Marker actives: Salidroside (= rhodioloside; 2-(4-hydroxyphenyl)ethyl beta-D-glucopyranoside), C14H20O7, MW 300.30 g/mol, CAS 10338-51-9. Rosavins are cinnamyl alcohol glycosides (rosavin C20H28O10 MW 428.4, plus rosin and rosarin); tyrosol (C8H10O2) is the salidroside aglycone. In the current formula Rhodiola appears ONLY in the dry capsules (variants A/B/C: 50/30/10 mg per 450 mg cap); it is NOT in any of the S2-S5/F5 liquids.

## 2. Three-rule findings
- **R1 solubility:** Marker compound salidroside water solubility = 60 mg/mL (~200 mM) at 25C per Selleck Chemicals datasheet (Tier 2 manufacturer monograph); Cayman/MedChemExpress SDS list PBS pH7.2 = 10 mg/mL, DMSO 20-60 mg/mL, ethanol 3 mg/mL (Tier 2). Whole standardized extract is a freely water-dispersible powder (PLT markets a water-soluble Rhodiola ingredient; Tier 3 marketing). INTENDED IN-SOLUTION CONCENTRATION FOR THIS FORMULA: effectively zero — Rhodiola lives in dry capsules, never in a liquid. If it were ever put in a drink: a human-equivalent dose is ~50 mg extract; at the bird's capsule level (10-50 mg/cap) dissolved in ~1 L drink that is ~0.01-0.05 mg/mL, vs salidroside solubility ceiling 60 mg/mL -> headroom >1000x -> nominally OK on pure solubility. CAVEAT: whole-extract solubility (not pure salidroside) is the relevant constant and is not cleanly quantified in a Tier-1 source -> the marker number is a proxy. Net R1 = OK (solubility is not the limiting factor for this substance).
- **R2 stability:** Salidroside is a phenylethanoid GLYCOSIDE: primary aqueous degradation pathway is hydrolytic cleavage of the glucoside bond to tyrosol (the aglycone) during storage; degradation follows first-order kinetics with rate constants ~3.9-33.3 x10^-3 /day depending on temperature/pH (Zhou et al., Osmanthus phenylethanoid glycoside degradation, Sci Rep 2017, PMID 28855701, Tier 1). Degradation is temperature- and pH-dependent and accelerated by heat. Powdered rosavins and salidroside are otherwise described as relatively resistant to thermal degradation as a dry powder (Tier 3 manufacturer/marketing). Manufacturer storage for pure salidroside: powder 3 years at -20C; stock solution in solvent ~1 month at -20C / 1 year at -80C (Selleck, Tier 2) — dry form shelf-stable for years but dissolved form explicitly short-lived. Stable-pH window vs formula pH ~5.8-6.2: glycoside hydrolysis is catalyzed at both acidic and basic extremes; mildly acidic near-neutral (pH ~4-6) is comparatively favorable but no published shelf-life at pH 5.8-6.2/25C exists (UNKNOWN, blocking gap if a liquid were required). Light/O2/metal: the tyrosol/phenolic moiety can oxidize; rosavins (cinnamyl) carry a UV-absorbing conjugated double bond -> photolabile. Net R2 for the DRY CAPSULE (its actual home): STABLE (no water, multi-year dry shelf-life). Net R2 for any hypothetical LIQUID: TIGHT-to-BLOCK (glycoside hydrolysis + phenolic oxidation + no validated aqueous shelf-life + photolability), reinforcing dry-only placement.
- **R3 conflicts:** Because Rhodiola is dry-capsule-only and NOT co-dissolved with any liquid ingredient, there are NO active in-solution interaction pathways in the current formula (only physical co-presence is with other dry capsule powders: creatine, glutamine, Mg-glycerophosphate, NAC, tyrosine, phenylalanine, P5P, arginine — all dry, no aqueous reaction). Hypothetical pairings if dissolved: (1) Rhodiola phenolics + divalent metals (Mg malate/MgCl2 in S5/F5, trace Fe/Cu) -> phenolics chelate metals and metals catalyze oxidation of the tyrosol/phenolic moiety; (2) Reducing-sugar matrices (S2 corn syrup/maltodextrin/ribose, fight-day dextrose) -> tyrosol is a phenol not an amine so classic Maillard is not the main risk, but phenolic browning/oxidation in a hot sugar matrix is plausible; (3) Pharmacodynamic stacking: Rhodiola is putatively MAO-inhibitory/CNS-stimulant; co-capsule with L-tyrosine + L-phenylalanine (catecholamine precursors) + Rhodiola could additively raise catecholaminergic tone -> physiological over-stimulation concern, NOT a chemistry precipitation issue; mitigation = note as pharmacodynamic caution, dose conservatively. Net R3: no real in-solution conflict (dry-only); pairwise risks are all avoided by keeping it out of the liquids.

## 3. Avian/poultry evidence
NO Rhodiola ROSEA poultry/avian efficacy or tolerance data found (searched broiler/layer growth/heat-stress/antioxidant literature — only other-phytogenic studies returned). The closest and only avian evidence is for a DIFFERENT species, Rhodiola IMBRICATA (Himalayan rhodiola, different rosavin/salidroside profile): Kalia et al. 2017, Indian J Anim Sci 87(10):1200-1206, DOI 10.56093/ijans.v87i10.75244 (Tier 1 peer-reviewed, NOT PubMed-indexed -> no PMID). Hydro-alcoholic R. imbricata extract in drinking water at 100/150/200/300/400/800 mg/kg body weight to broilers at high-altitude cold desert (3500 m): significantly increased total antioxidant capacity, free-radical scavenging, IL-2, total protein, globulin; decreased MDA, IL-6, cholesterol, triglyceride, LDL, glucose; best growth-performance effect at ~400 mg/kg BW. Supports a plausible antioxidant/anti-stress benefit class for the genus but does NOT validate R. rosea specifically, gives no defined max-tolerable/toxicity ceiling (800 mg/kg BW tested without reported toxicity but not framed as a NOAEL), and is chronic dietary (days-weeks), NOT an acute fight-day lever. No avian dose-response toxicity bound, no NRC entry, no avian salidroside/rosavin PK. Acute vs chronic: any benefit is CHRONIC (adaptogen over days), consistent with human data (200-600 mg/day standardized extract, weeks to effect; PMC9228580, Tier 1 review). BOTTOM LINE: effectively NO Rhodiola rosea avian data; only congener (R. imbricata) chronic dietary data exists.

## 4. Verdict
SKIP for the acute fight-day drink and per-round use case (no acute mechanism; adaptogens act over days, not within a six-round fight). ADD-conditional / demote-to-OPTIONAL only within the DRY route (capsule or preload powder) IF a multi-day pre-event chronic antioxidant program is genuinely run — and even then LOW priority because: (a) no R. rosea avian evidence (only wrong-species R. imbricata), (b) species/standardization mismatch, (c) preload already carries better-evidenced avian antioxidants/osmolytes (NAC, betaine, taurine, DMG). Concretely: in the winning two-SKU design KEEP Rhodiola OUT of the fight-day ORS drink entirely (SKIP), and treat as an OPTIONAL dry-preload add-on only. If used dry: target a human-equivalent ~25-50 mg standardized extract (3% rosavin/1% salidroside) per bird/day chronic, capped well below the R. imbricata 400 mg/kg-BW signal, marked LOW-CONFIDENCE optional. Never into any liquid SKU (R2 glycoside hydrolysis + photolability + no aqueous shelf-life). Aligns with the design direction's existing demotion of Rhodiola to optional.

## 5. Cautions
1) Species mismatch: the only avian data is Rhodiola IMBRICATA, not R. rosea — do not transfer the 400 mg/kg-BW efficacy/safety read-across uncritically. 2) Standardization variability: commercial R. rosea extracts vary widely in actual rosavin/salidroside content; adulteration/heavy-metal/pesticide residues documented on the US market (PMC12810810) — source a CoA-verified, marker-standardized extract. 3) Pharmacodynamic stacking: Rhodiola (putative MAO-inhibitory, CNS-stimulant) co-dosed with the capsule's L-tyrosine + L-phenylalanine (catecholamine precursors) could additively raise sympathetic/catecholaminergic tone — a welfare-relevant over-stimulation concern in an already stress/heat-loaded fighting bird; keep doses conservative. 4) No acute benefit: giving it on fight day between rounds is mechanistically futile (adaptogen needs days) — any inclusion must be chronic-preload framing, not acute. 5) Limited mammalian repro-safety data — relevant if breeding stock. 6) WELFARE NOTE (non-blocking): scope is the bird's own oxidative/stress resilience; a chronic antioxidant adaptogen is plausibly welfare-positive, but the R. rosea evidence base in chickens is essentially absent.

## 9. Blocking gaps / UNKNOWN
- UNKNOWN: no Rhodiola rosea (or any congener) acute/within-event ergogenic data in birds — the acute fight-day use case is unsupported by any species.
- UNKNOWN: no Rhodiola rosea avian efficacy, dose-response, or max-tolerable/toxicity (NOAEL) data — only wrong-species R. imbricata (Kalia 2017) exists; species read-across is a gap.
- UNKNOWN: no validated aqueous shelf-life or pH-stability curve for salidroside/rosavin at formula pH 5.8-6.2 and 25C — glycoside-hydrolysis half-life in this window is not published (BLOCK for any liquid SKU; non-issue for dry capsule).
- UNKNOWN: whole-extract (vs pure salidroside) water solubility lacks a Tier-1 quantitative monograph; the 60 mg/mL figure is for the pure marker only.
- LOW-CONFIDENCE: human dose context (200-600 mg/day) rests partly on Tier 2-3 sources; no avian-anchored target dose can be derived with confidence.
