# Substance: DMG (N,N-dimethylglycine)

# Substance Dossier: DMG (N,N-dimethylglycine)

## 1. Identity
N,N-dimethylglycine (DMG; dimethylaminoacetic acid; "vitamin B16" — non-vitamin trade name). Free acid CAS 1118-68-9, C4H9NO2, MW 103.12 g/mol, white crystalline zwitterion, mp 178-182 C. Common salt form for feed/aqueous use: sodium N,N-dimethylglycinate (Na-DMG), the form used in nearly all broiler trials (e.g. Taminco/Qualizyme DMG). Hydrochloride (DMG-HCl) also exists. Synonyms: dimethylglycine, DMG, N,N-dimethylaminoacetic acid. Naturally occurring intermediate in choline -> betaine -> glycine (one-carbon) metabolism. ChEBI:58251 (zwitterion). Tier 1/2: Wikipedia/Sigma-Aldrich D1156 monograph, ChemicalBook CB4440420.

## 2. Three-rule findings
- **R1 solubility:** Intended in-solution use: fight-day drink at 0.3-0.5 g/L (final), i.e. 0.3-0.5 mg/mL; per the winning design DMG is added to the dry ORS stick. Free-acid aqueous solubility ~50 mg/mL (~50 g/L) at room temp (Tier 2: Sigma-Aldrich D1156 / ChemicalBook CB4440420, reported "soluble in water 50 mg/mL"). The sodium salt (Na-DMG) is substantially more soluble than the free acid (ionic, hygroscopic — deliquesces in air; Tier 3 patent US11666071 notes strong hygroscopicity). Headroom: 50 mg/mL solubility / 0.5 mg/mL intended = ~100x. Verdict OK (>=3). Even at the higher historical wet-liquid concentrations (S4 DMG 10 g / 250 mL = 40 mg/mL; F5 5 g / 150 mL = 33 mg/mL) DMG stays below the ~50 mg/mL free-acid limit and well below Na-DMG saturation, so solubility was never the limiting issue for DMG — TIGHT only for the old S4 (40 vs 50 mg/mL = 1.25x headroom) which is a reason to prefer the salt form there; in the new dry-stick drink it is firmly OK.
- **R2 stability:** DMG is a small tertiary amino acid (zwitterion) with no hydrolyzable ester/amide bond, no oxidizable thiol, no conjugated chromophore -> chemically robust in water. No documented aqueous hydrolysis, oxidation, or photolysis pathway under formulation conditions (Tier 2 inference from structure + monograph; no degradation listed in Sigma/ChemicalBook hazard/stability data). pKa1 (COOH) ~1.9-2.04, pKa2 (N-H+) ~9.9 (Tier 2: ChEBI/Grokipedia/Wikipedia) -> at formula pH 5.8-6.2 (and at the dry-stick reconstituted pH ~4) DMG sits as the neutral zwitterion, stable across the entire 4-6.2 window; it provides essentially NO buffering near formula pH (buffering only within ~1 unit of its pKa values, i.e. <3 or >9), so it neither alkalinizes nor acidifies the drink — good for the near-neutral DEB target. Primary stability liability is PHYSICAL not chemical: both free acid and Na salt are highly HYGROSCOPIC and deliquesce — a dry premix MUST be packaged with desiccant in foil/opaque sachet, kept low-humidity (Tier 3 patent US11666071; Tier 2 ugent thesis). In the make-fresh dry-stick design, in-solution shelf-life is a non-issue (consumed same day). Realistic dry shelf-life 12-18 mo if moisture-protected. Not light-sensitive. Does NOT participate in Maillard browning at meaningful rate (tertiary amine, no free N-H on a primary amino group to condense with reducing sugars the way primary amino acids/lysine do) — a further reason it is safe alongside dextrose in the stick. Confirm: no preservative dependency because product is dry until use.
- **R3 conflicts:** DMG at 0.3-0.5 g/L is chemically inert toward the other fight-day-drink components (dextrose, NaCl, KCl, Na-citrate, citric acid, L-citrulline). Pair analysis: (1) DMG + reducing sugar (dextrose) -> mechanism: Maillard requires a primary or secondary amine free N-H; DMG's nitrogen is tertiary (fully methylated, no N-H once protonated/zwitterionic), so browning is negligible vs glucose -> mitigation: none needed; this is a key advantage over primary-amine actives. (2) DMG + citrate/citric acid -> no chelation (DMG is not a polydentate metal chelator at relevant pH) and no precipitation -> mitigation: none. (3) DMG + divalent metals (Mg, in legacy S5/feed) -> weak/no insoluble complex; DMG does not co-precipitate with Mg the way citrate+Ca/phosphate does -> mitigation: none, but Mg is moved to feed anyway. (4) DMG + L-citrulline -> both freely soluble, non-reactive, compatible in same dry stick. (5) Physiological/DEB -> DMG is electroneutral at drink pH, contributes ~0 to DEB, so it does not undermine the Cl-balanced near-neutral target. No redox conflict (no Fe/Cu-catalyzed oxidation of DMG; it is not an antioxidant that consumes during storage like ascorbate/NAC). Net: DMG is one of the most formulation-friendly actives in the set.

## 3. Avian/poultry evidence
STRONG, DIRECT broiler evidence (Tier 1). Poultry requirement: NOT a dietary requirement (endogenous choline-metabolism intermediate) — used as a functional feed additive, not an essential nutrient. Efficacy dose: 167 mg Na-DMG/kg feed significantly improved crude-protein/NFE digestibility AND cut pulmonary hypertension (ascites) incidence from 44.8% to 14.6% in cold-stressed high-energy broilers (Kalmar et al. 2010, Poult Sci / nutrient-digestibility & PHS attenuation study; PMID 20662970 is the PHS-finding paper; PMID 20666863 is the separate dose/fatty-acid-profile performance paper; recommended commercial dose 1000 mg Na-DMG/kg feed). Mechanism: one-carbon/methyl metabolism intermediate, improves fat/nutrient digestibility (lower jejunal pH, lower ileal viscosity), antioxidant/anti-lipid-peroxidation and endothelial support that abates pulmonary arterial hypertension/ascites syndrome — directly welfare- and recovery-relevant for an exerted bird. Max tolerable / safety: dedicated tolerance trial fed 0, 1, or 10 g Na-DMG/kg feed to 480 broilers over 39 d — safe and well tolerated, no adverse accumulation in edible tissues at the 1000 mg/kg recommended dose; 10x (10 g/kg) was the tolerated upper test level (Prinsen/Kalmar et al., Br J Nutr 2012, PMID 21929832). Dose-response on performance up to 1 g/kg with linear production-value gains and reduced abdominal fat in vegetal-fat diets (PMID 20666863). Acute vs chronic: avian benefit is documented as a CHRONIC dietary additive (days-weeks); no avian RCT shows benefit from a single acute drink sip — so DMG is at best a low-cost, freely-soluble, stable add to the acute drink (harmless, mild oxidative/heat-stress support) but its EVIDENCE-BASED home is chronic feed. Dose ranges (avian): 167-1000 mg Na-DMG/kg feed efficacious; up to 10 g/kg tolerated.

## 4. Verdict
ADD-conditional. Best placement: chronic DRY PRELOAD feed premix at ~150-1000 mg Na-DMG/kg feed (this is where the broiler evidence sits — antioxidant, anti-ascites/pulmonary-hypertension, digestibility). For the FIGHT-DAY DRINK, ADD-conditional at 0.3-0.5 g/L as the design proposes: it is freely soluble (~100x headroom), pH-stable, non-Maillard, non-chelating, DEB-neutral, and welfare-positive, so it is safe and cheap to include — but flag that NO avian study demonstrates acute single-session ergogenic benefit, so its presence in the drink is precautionary/low-confidence, not evidence-backed. SKIP all five legacy wet liquids (S4 40 mg/mL was near the free-acid solubility ceiling and depended on sorbate-at-pH-6 — both eliminated by the dry design). If forced to keep only one DMG entry, prioritize the chronic feed dose over the drink dose. Use the sodium salt (Na-DMG) for the dry premix (the studied form), packaged with desiccant.

## 5. Cautions
1) Acute drink inclusion is precautionary, NOT evidence-backed — avian benefit is a chronic-feeding effect; do not market the drink-DMG as an acute ergogenic. 2) Hygroscopicity/deliquescence of both free acid and Na salt is the real handling risk — dry premix and dry stick MUST use desiccant + foil/moisture barrier or it cakes/liquefies (welfare-neutral but producibility-critical). 3) "Vitamin B16" / pangamic-acid marketing lineage: DMG is sometimes conflated with calcium pangamate ("vitamin B15"), an ill-defined/adulterated product historically flagged by regulators — specify pharma-grade N,N-dimethylglycine (CAS 1118-68-9) or Na-DMG, NEVER "pangamate". 4) Repeated daily/high dosing: tolerance shown to 10 g/kg feed in broilers, so the proposed doses have a wide margin; the freely-excreted, choline-pathway nature means moderate excess is renally cleared (uricotelic) — low accumulation risk. 5) Welfare note (non-blocking): chronic DMG preload is welfare-POSITIVE (reduces pulmonary hypertension/ascites, supports antioxidant status in heat/cold stress) — squarely within the bird's own recovery/health scope.

## 9. Blocking gaps / UNKNOWN
- No peer-reviewed avian (or mammalian) RCT demonstrates ACUTE single-dose / single-session ergogenic or rehydration benefit of DMG in a drink — drink inclusion is low-confidence/precautionary (UNKNOWN acute effect).
- Exact aqueous solubility of the SODIUM salt (Na-DMG) in g/L at 25 C not found from a Tier 1/2 monograph — only qualitatively 'highly soluble/hygroscopic' (UNKNOWN precise Na-DMG saturation; not limiting since intended conc is ~0.5 g/L).
- Direct measured in-solution (aqueous) stability/shelf-life data for DMG at pH 4-6 over weeks not located in a primary source — stability inferred from structure + absence of reported degradation pathways (acceptable for dry/make-fresh design, but UNKNOWN for any forced wet-bottled SKU).
