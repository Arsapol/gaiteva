# Substance: Ascorbic Acid (Vitamin C)

# Substance Dossier: Ascorbic Acid (Vitamin C)

## 1. Identity
L-ascorbic acid; salt form sodium ascorbate (less acidic, adds Na). CAS 50-81-7. Antioxidant; birds synthesize it (kidney L-gulonolactone oxidase) but inadequately under acute stress.

## 2. Three-rule findings
- **R1 solubility:** ascorbic acid ~330 g/L; Na ascorbate higher. ✅ OK at 200 mg/L.
- **R2 stability:** OXIDIZES readily in solution (O2/light/heat, catalyzed by trace Fe/Cu) → dehydroascorbate/inactive. Acidic (free-acid form). → MAKE-FRESH at point of use, shade, give <1 h; amber/dark; minimize trace metals.
- **R3 conflicts:** trace-metal redox accelerates loss (chelator/citrate mildly protective); free-acid lowers pH (use Na ascorbate to spare acid load + watch Na budget).

## 3. Avian/poultry evidence
Dietary 500 mg/kg lowered plasma corticosterone + heart HSP70 under cyclic heat stress (Mahmoud, Edens et al. 2004 PMID 14698908, VERIFIED — first author Mahmoud, Edens co-author; cite accordingly). Acute anti-stress/oxidative support, ~150–300 mg/L water; NOT an ergogenic.

## 4. Verdict
ADD-conditional — FIGHT-DAY acute anti-stress; MUST be make-fresh (in-solution oxidation otherwise undercuts delivered dose).
