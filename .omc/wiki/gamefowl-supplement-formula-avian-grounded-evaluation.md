## KEY FRAMEWORK CORRECTIONS — human limits do NOT apply to birds
1. **B6**: human EFSA 12mg/day UL irrelevant. Poultry req 3-4.5mg/kg feed; safe to ~150-225mg/kg feed (≥50×); neurotoxic only ~1000× req. Judge per mg/kg feed, not human mg/day.
2. **Arginine**: birds lack urea cycle (no carbamoyl phosphate synthase-1) → STRICTLY essential, NRC req ~1.0-1.25% of diet (gram-scale). Capsule mg-dose is trivial. Citrulline DOES convert to arginine (valid sparing co-ingredient at gram scale). Mind Arg:Lys ratio.
3. **Taurine**: NOT essential in chickens (synthesize it; cat analogy wrong) — conditional/stress only.
4. **Acid-base is panting/respiratory-alkalosis driven**, not sweat/sodium. DEB (Na+K−Cl) is the most avian-rational lever; citrate = alkalinizing (→bicarbonate). Optimal DEB ~150-300 mEq/kg feed.
5. **Mg limit is enteric (osmotic laxation/wet litter) at ~0.3-0.5% feed (3000-5000mg/kg)**, not a blood-Mg neuromuscular ceiling.
6. **Creatine works in birds** despite endogenous synthesis (grain diets creatine-poor); 1200mg/kg feed raises muscle PCr. GAA (guanidinoacetic acid) is superior form (stable, cheap, arginine-sparing).
7. **Beta-alanine = chronic-only** (carnosine accretion over weeks, co-needs histidine); avian muscle already carnosine/anserine-rich.
8. **D-ribose = NO peer-reviewed avian skeletal-muscle evidence.** Only cardiac/ascites patent claims.
9. **DMG, NAC, betaine = avian-justified** (heat-stress, Nrf2 antioxidant, ascites/pulmonary-HTN protection, osmolyte).

## Gamefowl physiology
Fighting-cock muscle = fast-glycolytic type IIb burst, glycogen-fueled, lactate-limited. Inter-bout recovery limited by PCr/glycogen depletion + acidosis + THERMOREGULATION (panting). Highest-confidence levers: DEB/alkali > creatine/GAA preload > betaine > arginine(%-scale) > antioxidants(NAC/DMG/Mg).

## FINAL VERDICT (3-way locked)
**Acute-safe-on-the-numbers for occasional fight-day use.** Strongest rationale: DEB/alkali + citrulline/betaine/NAC-DMG support, matching fast-glycolytic recovery physiology. All earlier human-framed alarms (B6 toxicity, Mg excess, osmotic load, "pharmacologic bolus") DISSOLVE at measured doses.

**Improvements:** (1) cut/reduce D-ribose (only real waste); (2) add Cl to control DEB if dosed daily long-term; (3) creatine/beta-alanine → preload weeks ahead, not fight-day; consider GAA over creatine; (4) sum B6 across ALL sources (S3 + S4 B-complex + capsule P5P) to keep <~20mg/day.

**Caveats:** n=1 observational no control; B6 total excludes hidden S4/capsule sources; DEB mEq not directly comparable to feed mEq/kg without total daily intake; no gamefowl-specific (vs broiler) controlled trials exist.
