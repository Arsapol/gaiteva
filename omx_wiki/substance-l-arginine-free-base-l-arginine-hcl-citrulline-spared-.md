# Substance: L-Arginine (free base + L-arginine-HCl; citrulline-spared route)

# Substance Dossier: L-Arginine (free base + L-arginine-HCl; citrulline-spared route)

## 1. Identity
L-Arginine, the strictly dietary-essential basic amino acid in birds. Free base: CAS 74-79-3, MW 174.20, C6H14N4O2. Hydrochloride salt: L-arginine monohydrochloride, CAS 1119-34-2, MW 210.66. Synonyms: (S)-2-amino-5-guanidinopentanoic acid, Arg, R. pKa ~2.17 (COOH), ~9.04 (alpha-NH3+), ~12.48 (guanidinium) -> isoelectric point ~10.76; the guanidinium group remains protonated/positively charged across the entire physiological and formula pH range [Wikipedia/Arginine, Tier 2; Fitch et al. "Arginine: Its pKa value revisited," Protein Sci 2015 DOI 10.1002/pro.2647, Tier 1]. In this formula L-arginine appears ONLY in the S1 FOCUS capsule at ~18-27.5 mg/cap (dry, not in solution). All liquid NO-axis support is delivered as citrulline malate (S3 13 g, S4 1.5 g) — i.e., arginine is currently citrulline-spared in the wet products.

## 2. Three-rule findings
- **R1 solubility:** Intended in-solution concentration in the CURRENT formula = effectively 0 in any liquid (arginine is dry-capsule only); the citrulline-spared route means no free arginine is dissolved. If arginine were ever put into a drink, solubility is NOT the limiter: L-arginine free base ~148.7 g/L (14.87 g/100 mL) @20C [Wikipedia/Arginine citing CRC, Tier 2]; rising to ~400 g/L @50C and ~83 g/L @0C [ECHA registered dossier, Tier 2]. L-arginine-HCl is far more soluble, ~730 g/L (and a ResearchGate-cited "182 mg/mL" figure for the form used) [ECHA/ResearchGate, Tier 2-3]. Against any plausible drink use (<=1-2 g/L for a gram-scale NO lever), headroom = 148700/2000 ~74x for free base, far higher for HCl -> OK on pure solubility. CAVEAT: the free base does NOT dissolve as a neutral solution — see R2; the practical limiter is its alkalinity, not saturation. Verdict on R1 alone: OK (solubility ample for both salt forms).
- **R2 stability:** Solid L-arginine (free base and HCl) is stable, non-hygroscopic to mildly hygroscopic, long shelf-life dry -> fully compatible with the dry-preload / dry-capsule design. IN WATER the free base is the problem: arginine aqueous solutions are strongly ALKALINE (guanidinium pKa ~12.48; free base raises solution pH well above 9-10) and "tend to absorb CO2 from the atmosphere on standing"; recommendation is to store buffered, sealed, <1 month [ResearchGate "How stable is L-arginine on aqueous solution," Tier 3; consistent with pKa data Tier 1]. This strongly alkaline pH directly fights the formula target of ~5.8-6.2 and the new near-neutral Cl-balanced drink design — adding free-base arginine would alkalinize the drink (the exact over-alkalinization error the redesign removed) and push toward the panting respiratory-alkalosis overshoot the avian frame warns against. Degradation pathways: alkaline conditions catalyze Maillard browning with reducing sugars (browning of arginine-supplemented parenteral/aqueous solutions is documented; alkaline pH promotes reactive carbonyl/Amadori formation) [Maillard's reaction in parenteral solutions supplemented with arginine, ve.scielo S0004-06222001000300008, Tier 2; Maillard review PMC12154226, Tier 1]; slow alkaline hydrolysis of the guanidino group to ornithine + urea under harsh conditions (not relevant at room-temp neutral pH but accelerated by heat/alkali) [Hydrolysis of Arginine, J Biol Chem classic, Tier 1]. L-arginine-HCl buffers nearer neutral (pH ~5.5-7 for ~200 g/L) [ECHA, Tier 2] and would be the only acceptable salt if a liquid were ever required. Stable pH window vs formula ~5.8-6.2: the HCl salt is compatible; the free base is NOT (self-alkalinizing). Net: dry = excellent stability; wet free-base = poor (alkalinizing + Maillard + CO2 uptake, <1 mo). Keep arginine dry.
- **R3 conflicts:** (1) Arginine free base + reducing sugars (D-ribose S2/S5/F5, corn syrup + maltodextrin S2) -> Maillard browning, accelerated by arginine's own alkalinity and any heat; loss of both arginine and sugar, brown pigments [ve.scielo S0004-06222001000300008, Tier 2; Maillard review PMC12154226, Tier 1]. Mitigation: keep arginine dry and physically separated from the dextrose/ribose sugar matrix; the redesign already deletes ribose/corn syrup, which removes this pair. (2) Arginine free base + acidic/buffered drink (citric acid, citrate, ascorbate) -> acid-base conflict: arginine raises pH, the citrate/citric system is the buffer; net alkalinization opposes the near-neutral Cl-balanced DEB target. Mitigation: if any arginine is dissolved, use L-arginine-HCl (contributes Cl, near-neutral) NOT free base; better, keep it dry and use free L-citrulline as the soluble acute lever instead. (3) Lysine-arginine antagonism (physiological, not chemical): high dietary lysine up-regulates renal arginase and inhibits arginine tubular reabsorption, increasing arginine catabolism/excretion; also Ile/Phe/His/Tyr raise kidney arginase (relevant — the capsules carry L-tyrosine 50-110 mg and L-phenylalanine 10-60 mg) [Involvement of food intake in the lysine-arginine antagonism in chicks, PMID 1159527, Tier 1; Frontiers/PMC10791986 review, Tier 1]. Mitigation: keep an arginine:lysine ratio near 1.0-1.18 (NRC) in the chronic feed base; the acute capsule doses are tiny so antagonism is a feed-formulation note, not an acute drink issue. (4) Substitution conflict / redundancy with citrulline malate (S3/S4): citrulline -> arginine via argininosuccinate synthase/lyase bypasses hepatic first-pass and raises plasma arginine MORE efficiently per gram than oral arginine itself [Supplemental citrulline more efficient than arginine in increasing systemic arginine availability, J Nutr, Tier 1; Citrulline improves arginine availability under enhanced arginase, MDPI Nutrients 7(7):5217, Tier 1]. Mechanism -> mitigation: prefer free L-citrulline as the soluble NO-axis lever (the redesign already does this), reserve free arginine for the DRY preload only; do NOT double-dose both at gram scale or you stack the same axis. Note: citrulline-MALATE adds malate (alkali load) — the redesign switches to free L-citrulline; arginine itself adds no malate.

## 3. Avian/poultry evidence
Arginine is STRICTLY dietary-essential in birds: chickens lack a functional urea cycle (incomplete; deficient carbamoyl phosphate synthase-1 / ornithine cycle) so cannot synthesize arginine de novo and depend on diet [Beyond protein synthesis: emerging role of arginine in poultry nutrition, PMC10791986 / Front Physiol 2023 DOI 10.3389/fphys.2023.1326809, Tier 1; Merck Vet Manual, Nutritional Requirements of Poultry, Tier 1]. Poultry CAN convert citrulline -> arginine via argininosuccinate synthase + lyase, validating the citrulline-sparing route [PMC10791986, Tier 1]. POULTRY REQUIREMENT (NRC Nutrient Requirements of Poultry, 1994): broiler arginine ~1.25% diet (0-3 wk), 1.10% (3-6 wk), 1.00% (6-8 wk); recommended Arg:Lys ratio ~1.14 / 1.10 / 1.18 [NRC 1994 via Merck/Frontiers reviews, Tier 1]. DOSE RANGES with avian outcomes: supplemental +1.0% dietary L-arginine-HCl significantly reduced cumulative pulmonary hypertension syndrome (ascites) mortality in broilers [PMID 7724455 (Wideman), Tier 1] and preserved endothelial NOS expression in pulmonary arterioles of cold-exposed broilers, attenuating PHS [PMID 16169754, Tier 1]; arginine + vitamin E improved cardiopulmonary/ascites parameters after epinephrine challenge [Oxford Poultry Science 85(12):2241, Tier 1]. Excess over 1994 NRC improved growth and immune status in broilers [ScienceDirect S1871141318300015, Tier 1]. MAX TOLERABLE: arginine is freely catabolized/renally handled (uricotelic); excess is largely cleared, BUT chronic gross excess provokes the lysine-arginine antagonism (renal arginase up-regulation) rather than direct toxicity [PMID 1159527, Tier 1]; no discrete neurotoxic ceiling like B6. ACUTE vs CHRONIC: the documented avian benefits (PHS/ascites protection, eNOS, immunity, growth) are CHRONIC dietary-loading effects over days-weeks, not single-sip acute ergogenics — consistent with placing arginine/citrulline in the chronic preload, with citrulline as the only justified gram-scale acute add.

## 4. Verdict
ADD-conditional, DRY ONLY. (1) PRELOAD (feed/dry, ~7-10 d pre-event): defensible to include arginine — but the more efficient, first-pass-sparing, non-alkalinizing choice is free L-citrulline (citrulline-spared route), so arginine itself is OPTIONAL/secondary here; if used, L-arginine-HCl or free base dry, target chronic Arg:Lys ~1.0-1.18 (NRC), and do NOT gram-stack with citrulline on the same axis. Chronic avian benefit (PHS/ascites protection, eNOS, immunity) is real [PMID 7724455, 16169754]. (2) FIGHT-DAY DRINK: prefer free L-citrulline 1.0-1.3 g/L as the acute lever (already in redesign); SKIP free-base L-arginine in the drink (self-alkalinizing, Maillard with any sugar, fights near-neutral DEB target). If a soluble arginine were ever mandated, use L-arginine-HCl only (near-neutral, contributes Cl), never free base. (3) REVISED LIQUIDS (old S3/S4 citrulline malate): SKIP the malate form (adds alkali) -> switch to free L-citrulline per redesign. Net: arginine = ADD-conditional to dry preload as an optional adjunct; the citrulline-spared route is preferred and already implemented.

## 5. Cautions
Welfare/scope note (non-blocking): arginine/citrulline NO-axis support is welfare-POSITIVE for the bird's own physiology — it lowers pulmonary hypertension/ascites risk and supports heat-stress cardiopulmonary function [PMID 7724455, 16169754]. Genuine cautions to record as written notes: (a) Free-base arginine in any wet product is self-alkalinizing (pH >9 in concentrate), absorbs CO2, <1 month aqueous shelf — a real producibility/stability hazard; keep dry. (b) Maillard browning risk if arginine ever contacts reducing sugars (ribose/dextrose/corn syrup) in solution + warmth — co-packing must be avoided. (c) Lysine-arginine antagonism: at gross dietary arginine excess (or conversely high lysine) renal arginase rises and arginine is wasted; keep Arg:Lys ratio in NRC range in the chronic feed — relevant because capsules also carry Tyr/Phe which raise kidney arginase. (d) Do not double-dose arginine AND citrulline at gram scale on the same NO axis — redundant and may over-vasodilate; choose citrulline as the primary lever. (e) Repeated daily (vs occasional event) use shifts the risk profile; benefits cited are chronic-loading, occasional/event framing assumed. (f) NO acute single-sip ergogenic evidence for arginine in birds — acute drink inclusion is not avian-justified; its place is the chronic preload.
