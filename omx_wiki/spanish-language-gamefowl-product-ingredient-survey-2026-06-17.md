## Ingredient pattern summary

1. **B12-centered oxygenation/recovery cluster:** B12 alone or “triple B12”, B12 + iron, B12 + B15, B12 + butafosfan.
2. **B15/pangamic-acid cluster:** B15/sodium pangamate or pangamic acid with magnesium/potassium aspartates and B vitamins.
3. **Energy/ATP cluster:** ATP, creatine, L-carnitine, L-arginine, maltodextrin/carbohydrate.
4. **Stimulant/botanical cluster:** caffeine, guaraná, green tea, Panax ginseng, maca, tyrosine, taurine; strongest example is Ener-Jet.
5. **Mineral/hematopoietic cluster:** iron, phosphorus/butafosfan, selenium, Mg/K aspartates.

## Safety-review flags for future work

- **Stimulant stack risk:** caffeine + guaraná + green-tea extract + ginseng + tyrosine/phenylamine-style ingredients could be over-stimulating; no concentration disclosed.
- **Iron/selenium:** useful market signal but narrow safety margins in birds; requires exact mg per serving and total-diet accounting.
- **B15/pangamic acid:** popular in Spanish products, but evidence/regulatory status and true identity may vary by supplier.
- **Injectable formulas:** Cacodil B-15 and Butasol-like products include sterile-drug assumptions and sometimes lidocaine; do not copy into oral/sublingual formula without redesign.
- **Osmolality/pH:** liquid stimulant formulas with sugars, amino acids, salts, caffeine extracts, and acids may be hyperosmolar. If making an oral/sublingual analog, measure osmolality and pH rather than calculate only.

## Open gaps
