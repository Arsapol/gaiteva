# Substance: Coenzyme Q10 (ubiquinone / CoQ10)

## 1. Identity
Coenzyme Q10 (CoQ10, ubiquinone-10, ubidecarenone), CAS 303-98-0, lipid-soluble benzoquinone/isoprenoid mitochondrial electron-transfer cofactor and antioxidant. Candidate use in Gaiteva V3: oil-phase addition to the MCT emulsion at **100 mg/100 mL** (≈5 mg per 5 mL dose), or dry/oil premix if emulsion assay fails.

## 2. Three-rule findings
- **R1 solubility:** Practically water-insoluble / lipid-soluble. In a water phase this is a BLOCK; in the proposed MCT oil phase it is an oil-dispersed active. Intended: 100 mg/20 mL oil phase = 5 mg/mL oil-phase loading before emulsification. Exact solubility in the chosen MCT oil is **UNKNOWN — must verify with supplier/lab clarity test**; if undissolved crystals remain, use micronized suspension or lower dose.
- **R2 stability:** Light/oxygen/heat sensitive enough to require amber/opaque packaging, low-oxygen headspace if possible, and antioxidant co-formulant (vitamin E). CoQ10 finished-product stability is formulation-dependent; 6–12 month label potency requires HPLC assay, not assumption.
- **R3 conflicts:** No Maillard conflict; no aqueous redox cycling like ascorbate. Main conflict is physical: crystallization/phase separation in oil-in-water emulsion and oxidation under light/O2. Compatible conceptually with MCT + vitamin E acetate.

## 3. Avian/poultry evidence
Direct chicken heat-stress evidence exists: Kikusato et al. 2016 (PMID 26707541; DOI 10.1111/asj.12543) reported **40 mg/kg diet CoQ10** suppressed heat-stress-induced mitochondrial ROS overproduction and oxidative damage in chicken skeletal muscle. This is chronic dietary evidence, not proof that a single 5 mg oral bolus has acute performance benefit.

## 4. Verdict for our use
**ADD-conditional** to the MCT oil phase for antioxidant/mitochondrial score improvement, but **do not count as an acute stimulant**. Best role: mitochondrial oxidative-stress protection and oil-phase antioxidant synergy. Production blocker: measured CoQ10 solubility/distribution and potency retention in the exact emulsion.

## Update 2026-06-16 — independent verification

Independent verifier `Cicero` returned **CONFIRMED with caveats**.

- PMID 26707541 / DOI 10.1111/asj.12543 directly supports that dietary CoQ10 at **40 mg/kg diet** suppressed heat-stress mitochondrial ROS/oxidative damage in chicken skeletal muscle.
- Proposed **5 mg per 5 mL dose** is in the same order as daily dietary exposure for a 2.5 kg bird eating ~100–125 g feed/day, but it is **not proven as an acute fight-day ergogenic**.
- R1: no aqueous solubility blocker if CoQ10 is held in the MCT/oil phase; proposed oil-phase loading is low.
- R2: light/oxygen/heat sensitive; amber/low-O2 packaging and HPLC assay required.

Verifier verdict: **CoQ10 = ADD-conditional / confirmed as mitochondrial antioxidant support; production still needs finished-emulsion stability and potency testing.**
