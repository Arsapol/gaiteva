# Gamefowl Formula V3 — High-Score Hybrid Core Emulsion + Dry Activator

## Decision summary
To beat Route A's stats without destroying the 6–12 month shelf-life target, the winning architecture is **not a pure wet all-in-one bottle**. It is a **hybrid**:

1. **Bottle A: shelf-stable MCT/ribose/DMG/LCLT core emulsion** with oil-soluble CoQ10 + vitamin E acetate.
2. **Dry Activator B: focus/recovery sachet or dry cap** mixed or given at use time; keeps tyrosine, B6, vitamin C, taurine/betaine/creatine-like supports out of wet storage.

This preserves the user's "single product / nearly everything" goal while avoiding the wet matrix that blocks 6–12 month stability.

## Formula draft

### Bottle A — V3 Core Emulsion, per 100 mL
| Ingredient | Amount / 100 mL | Role |
|---|---:|---|
| MCT oil | 20 mL | energy-dense oil phase; lower than 30–40% to improve GI/emulsion stability |
| D-ribose | 6 g | field-supported recovery/ATP-pathway lever; kept away from primary amines in the stored bottle |
| DMG HCl or Na-DMG equivalent | 2 g | avian-relevant heat/oxidative/pulmonary-stress support; formulation-friendly |
| L-carnitine L-tartrate | 1 g | chronic fat-metabolism/cardiopulmonary support; stable in solution |
| CoQ10 / ubiquinone | 100 mg | mitochondrial antioxidant; oil-phase active |
| Vitamin E acetate | 200 mg | lipid-soluble antioxidant; protects oil phase and raises heat-stress antioxidant score |
| Magnesium chloride hexahydrate | 0.6–1.0 g | Mg support + chloride; specify salt form and calculate elemental Mg |
| NaCl | 0.4–0.6 g | chloride-balanced electrolyte; avoid citrate-only alkalinization |
| KCl | 0.15–0.25 g | potassium + chloride |
| Sodium citrate dihydrate | 0.2–0.3 g | small buffer only, not heavy alkalinizer |
| Citric acid | q.s. pH 4.0–4.5 | preservative efficacy / pH control |
| Lecithin liquid | ~3–4 mL | primary emulsifier |
| Polysorbate 80 | ~1–1.5 mL | co-emulsifier |
| Glycerin | ~3 mL | gum dispersion / mouthfeel |
| Xanthan + guar | 0.05 g + 0.05 g | suspension; keep low for syringeability |
| Preservative | validated at final pH | example benzoate/sorbate system; must pass challenge test |
| Distilled water | q.s. to 100 mL | aqueous phase |

Per 5 mL dose this delivers roughly: MCT 1 mL; ribose 300 mg; DMG 100 mg; LCLT 50 mg; CoQ10 5 mg; vitamin E acetate 10 mg; plus modest electrolytes.

### Dry Activator B — per event dose or sachet
| Ingredient | Working dose | Role |
|---|---:|---|
| L-tyrosine | 100–250 mg | focus / catecholamine precursor; dry only |
| Pyridoxine-HCl or P5P | 2–3 mg B6-equivalent | catecholamine/amino-acid metabolism cofactor; dry only; keep total B6 <15 mg/session |
| Ascorbic acid or sodium ascorbate | 50–100 mg | acute anti-stress antioxidant / DBH cofactor; make-fresh only |
| Taurine | 100–150 mg | cardiac/osmolyte/heat-stress support; dry avoids ribose Maillard storage |
| Betaine anhydrous | 200–300 mg | osmolyte / methyl support; dry cap or separate preload |
| Creatine monohydrate or GAA | 150–250 mg creatine OR preload GAA | ATP/PCr score; works best as 7–10 day preload, not single acute dose |
| Optional TBCC copper | 0.05–0.1 mg elemental Cu | DBH cofactor; only if precise micro-dosing is feasible |

## GO / NO-GO
**GO-CONDITIONAL for prototype V3 hybrid.**
**NO-GO for a pure 6–12 month wet all-in-one bottle containing the dry activator substances.**

## Blocking gaps before production
- CoQ10 solubility/distribution in the exact MCT oil/emulsion is UNKNOWN; requires lab clarity/particle and HPLC potency testing.
- Preservative system must be validated at actual pH 4.0–4.5 by microbial challenge test.
- 6–12 month emulsion stability requires centrifuge, heat/cool, freeze/thaw, rancidity/peroxide, pH drift, viscosity, color/browning, and potency tests.
- Acute tyrosine focus benefit has no direct avian bolus trial; field score must track calm alertness, not over-arousal.

## Cautions
- Do not market as pain tolerance or injury masking; use fatigue/stress tolerance and recovery support.
- Competition/regulatory status of all substances must be checked by owner for target jurisdiction.
- Multi-day preload ingredients should not be credited as instant effects if given only once.

## Update (2026-06-16T16:38:11.217Z)

## Update 2026-06-16 — verifier corrections applied

Independent verifier `Cicero` checked the two new oil-phase additions.

### Confirmed / corrected
- **CoQ10:** CONFIRMED with caveats. The chicken heat-stress mitochondrial ROS source (PMID 26707541; DOI 10.1111/asj.12543) supports the direction. Proposed 5 mg/dose is not an acute ergogenic proof; it is mitochondrial/oxidative-stress support.
- **Vitamin E acetate:** CORRECTED. It is a plausible stable vitamin E source and no dose red flag was found at ~10 mg/dose, but it should **not** be claimed as strong in-bottle MCT oil protection. If oil protection is a goal, evaluate mixed tocopherols/free tocopherol/ascorbyl palmitate as excipients separately.

### Production readiness correction
- **6-month shelf life:** plausible only after validated emulsifier/preservative/amber or airless packaging testing.
- **12-month shelf life:** not confirmed without finished-product stability data.
- Required tests: phase separation, droplet size, pH drift, preservative challenge, peroxide/anisidine values, rancidity/smell, color/browning, viscosity/syringeability, and HPLC assay for CoQ10/vitamin E.

Stats-card shelf score remains **7.0–7.5 prototype**, but commercial-readiness should be capped until these tests pass.
