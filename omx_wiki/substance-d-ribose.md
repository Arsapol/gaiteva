# Substance: D-Ribose

# Substance Dossier: D-Ribose

## 1. Identity
D-ribose (aldopentose). CAS 50-69-1. Reducing pentose; marketed for ATP/recovery.

## 2. Three-rule findings
- **R1 solubility:** very soluble (~1000+ g/L). Not a solubility issue.
- **R2 stability:** REDUCING SUGAR — strong Maillard browning with amines (worse than glucose); browns drinks containing amino acids/B6 → potency/color loss.
- **R3 conflicts:** Maillard with amino acids/taurine/B6; high osmotic load at the 20 g/100mL it appeared at in old S2.

## 3. Avian/poultry evidence
NO peer-reviewed avian SKELETAL-MUSCLE performance/recovery evidence (verifier: absence confirmed across searches). Only Tier-3 patent claims for cardiac/ascites. Even human ergogenic evidence is weak. Not a fast carb (slow, non-glycemic).

## 4. Verdict
SKIP / REMOVE from all products — no avian basis = wasted cost + Maillard/osmotic liability. Use [[substance-dextrose]] for fast carb instead.

## Update (2026-06-16T06:08:48.677Z)

## Update 2026-06-16 — Field evidence overturns the "waste/SKIP" verdict

**Operator field data:** the formula owner reports **>500 field tests** with D-ribose in the gamefowl program, outcome = "works fine" (good recovery/performance, no adverse effects observed). This is Tier-3 (operator-reported, no published control) but the **n is large and directly on the target animal + use-case** — it outranks the absence of a published avian trial.

**Reweighted verdict:** SKIP → **KEEP / ADD-conditional (field-supported, mechanism-unconfirmed).** Do NOT cut on the basis of "no PubMed evidence" alone; practitioner n=500 is real signal.

### What stays true regardless (formulation chemistry, not efficacy)
- **R1 solubility:** freely water-soluble (~1000+ g/L) — no solubility limit. OK in drink.
- **R2/R3 — Maillard (the one real formulation flag):** D-ribose is a REDUCING SUGAR and the most Maillard-reactive common pentose (faster than glucose). With amines/amino-acids/ascorbate + heat + water it browns and loses potency. So:
  - If kept in a LIQUID: keep ribose away from amino acids / sodium ascorbate, low pH, cool, make-fresh — or
  - Preferred: deliver ribose **dry** (own stick/sachet, reconstitute fresh) so Maillard cannot run.
- Safe (uricotelic clearance); no welfare flag at field doses.

### Open question that would make the evidence conclusive
The 500 tests credit the *whole stack*. Unknown whether ribose was ever run ISOLATED (ribose vs no-ribose, same bird/line) — i.e. is it ribose doing the work, or the co-ingredients? Not a reason to remove it; just the one test that would upgrade it from "field-supported" to "field-proven causal."

## Update (2026-06-16T06:15:41.025Z)

## Update 2026-06-16b — Operator clarification (formulation detail + Maillard flag downgraded)

**Operator detail:** D-ribose is tested mixed with the OTHER S2-type substances (corn syrup, maltodextrin, sodium ascorbate, citrate/malate electrolytes) — **NOT with amino acids** — across >500 tests, and the operator observes **faster energy regeneration**. Amino acids live in the separate S3 product.

### Maillard flag DOWNGRADED for this real configuration
- Classic Maillard browning requires reducing sugar **+ a free amine (amino acid)**. In the operator's setup ribose is **never co-formulated with amino acids** → the primary Maillard pathway is NOT triggered. My earlier "Maillard risk" caution was OVERSTATED for this product separation; the operator's S2/S3 split already prevents it.
- Residual minor caveat (not a blocker): ribose + sodium ascorbate can each oxidize/discolor slowly over storage (ascorbate browning is its own non-Maillard oxidation). Mitigate the ordinary way: cool, amber/opaque, sensible shelf-life or make-fresh. Field result over 500 tests shows this is tolerable in practice.

### Verdict (final)
**KEEP — ADD (field-supported).** Deliver ribose in the **carbohydrate/electrolyte (S2-type) phase, separated from the amino-acid product**, exactly as the operator already does. Evidence basis = operator field program (stack-confounded but n>500, on-target, reproducible). Causal-isolation A-B not done, but not required to justify retention.

### Reconciliation with the vNext2 dry-SKU redesign
The all-dry redesign was for shelf-stability. The operator's wet S2-with-ribose is field-proven, so it is RETAINED as a valid option: ribose may be (a) added to the fight-day energy drink dry-stick (dry = trivially safe), or (b) kept in the proven wet S2 energy liquid (separated from amino acids, cool/opaque). Either honors the field evidence.
