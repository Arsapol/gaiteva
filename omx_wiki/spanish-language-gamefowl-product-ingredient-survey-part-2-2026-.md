## New pattern findings from this round

1. **Tornel cluster is very B12/B15/ATP/aspartate-heavy:** Super B12 5500, Top B15+3, Pangamine-250, Sure Shot, Super Performance-L.
2. **B15/pangamate is repeatedly marketed as “oxygenation / fatigue / recovery”** across Tornel, Econovet, Pharvet, Vitofarma-style products.
3. **Nucleotide energy pattern appears repeatedly:** ATP alone, ATP + UTP, ATP + ribose, ATP + B12/B15.
4. **Hematopoietic/mineral products often include arsenical cacodylate plus iron/cobalt/B12** (Gallofos, Hematofos, Hemoplex). This is a high-caution drug/mineral class, not a simple supplement template.
5. **Dry oral capsules/tablets can carry complex minerals/vitamins more easily than wet liquids** (Antoplex), avoiding precipitation/Maillard/microbial issues.
6. **Stimulant products remain the least transparent:** Ener-Jet lists many stimulants, while Mamba/Testo-style products often provide marketing claims but not quantitative formula.

## Formula-review implications

- For a humane/legal oral wellness product, the safer design lessons are **B12-form comparison, low-dose L-carnitine/creatine/arginine concepts, ribose/ATP marketing pattern, and dry-capsule mineral separation**.
- Avoid copying injectable formulas into oral/sublingual form because sterile route, excipients, pH, preservatives, and tissue exposure differ.
- Treat **selenium, iron, copper, iodine, arsenical cacodylate, benzyl alcohol, lidocaine, caffeine/guaraná/green tea** as hard safety-review items before any formula work.
- For any liquid analog, measure actual **pH and osmolality**; many listed substances can push osmolality high.
