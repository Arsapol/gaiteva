# Substance: Potassium Chloride (KCl)

# Substance Dossier: Potassium Chloride (KCl)

## 1. Identity
Potassium chloride. CAS 7447-40-7. K+ source that ALSO supplies Cl− → DEB-balanced (chosen over K-citrate to avoid alkalinization).

## 2. Three-rule findings
- **R1 solubility:** ~340 g/L (25°C, verified). At ~1.3 g/L → ✅ OK.
- **R2 stability:** fully stable.
- **R3 conflicts:** none. Unlike K-citrate (alkalinizing, 3 HCO3−/citrate), KCl is acid-base neutral and supplies the Cl− that holds DEB near-neutral.

## 3. Avian/poultry evidence
NRC broiler K req ~0.30%; K well-tolerated (renally excreted) but high K drives water intake. DEB relevance: K is a + ion in Na+K−Cl. Using KCl (not citrate) is the v-next fix that keeps DEB near-neutral in a panting (alkalosis-prone) bird.

## 4. Verdict
ADD-strong — FIGHT-DAY K source; REPLACES K-citrate (critic m3/M1 fix). Caution: hyperkalemia risk in a dehydrated bird if grossly overdosed; specify "K+ mM" basis.
