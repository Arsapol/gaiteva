# Substance: Betaine Anhydrous

# Substance Dossier: Betaine Anhydrous

## 1. Identity
Betaine (trimethylglycine, glycine betaine). CAS 107-43-7. Quaternary-ammonium zwitterion; organic osmolyte + methyl donor. (Also betaine HCl form.)

## 2. Three-rule findings
- **R1 solubility:** very high ~1600 g/L (~160 g/100g water, verified). ✅ OK at any drink dose.
- **R2 stability:** stable zwitterion in solution; not heat/oxidation labile.
- **R3 conflicts:** NOT a Maillard substrate (no free primary amine) → safe with dextrose. Provides methyl groups (synergy with GAA's methyl demand).

## 3. Avian/poultry evidence
Avian osmolyte: improved water retention + acid-base in heat-stressed broiler oral-rehydration fluid (Sayed & Downing 2011 PMID 21177455, VERIFIED). ~1 g/kg feed well-supported; 0.5–1.5 g/L water (upper end may be supraoptimal at peak heat/high water intake — verifier). Also methyl-donor (choline/methionine sparing) + cell-volume protection.

## 4. Verdict
ADD-conditional (lean strong) — FIGHT-DAY hydration aid + PRELOAD methyl-donor pairing with GAA.
