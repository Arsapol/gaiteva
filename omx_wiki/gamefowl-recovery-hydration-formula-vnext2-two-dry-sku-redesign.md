# Gamefowl Recovery/Hydration Formula vNext — Two Dry-SKU Producibility-First Redesign (PRELOAD + Fight-Day SGLT1 ORS)

## Scope / ethics
Research framing: the BIRD's own recovery/hydration/health (welfare-positive, dual-use). Genuine welfare/safety concerns recorded as written Cautions, not used to block research (per user directive).

## Winning approach
**producibility-stability-first** (score 39/50; tied with field-performance, won on manufacturability). Master move: collapse 5 wet liquids (S2,S3,S4,S5,F5) into **TWO DRY SKUs** + retain dry capsule. Dry-first strongly reduces (codex: not literally eliminates) hydrolysis, Maillard browning, microbial growth, NAC thiol oxidation, B12/P5P photolysis, AND the BLOCK-severity K-sorbate-at-pH-6 preservative defect at once — no preservative needed.

## GO / NO-GO
GO — two producible, stable dry SKUs; every cap met by construction (B6 0 mg/session, Mg 0 mg/session in drink; osm 258 mOsm/kg delivered; DEB +3.06 mEq/L near-neutral); no unmitigated Maillard/redox/precipitation; no preservative-at-pH-6 defect.

## Final formula
## Architecture: five wet liquids (S2–S5, F5) collapse into TWO DRY products + retained dry capsule

Dry-first is the master move: removing water eliminates hydrolysis, Maillard browning, microbial growth, cold chain, the photolysis of B12/P5P, the thiol-oxidation of NAC, AND the BLOCK-severity K-sorbate-at-pH-6 preservative defect — all at once. No preservative is needed in either dry SKU.

**VERIFIED KEY NUMBERS (recomputed this session):**
- glucose:Na molar = **2.11** (near SGLT1 optimum ~1.8; chicken jejunal SGLT1 Shibata 2023 PMID 37321034 — mechanistic inference, absorption-ontogeny endpoint)
- Na 52.7 mM, K 14.8 mM, Cl **64.4 mM**
- **Osmolality ~258 mOsm/kg (delivered)** — HYPOTONIC, <300 cap met, promotes net water absorption
- **DEB = +3.06 mEq/L** — NEAR-NEUTRAL, Cl-balanced (corrects old over-alkalinizing citrate stack)

Mixing instruction (non-chemist): "Empty one stick into 1 L cool water, stir 30 s until clear, offer fresh; discard after the session / within the day." **Shelf-life: dry stick 12–18 mo; reconstituted = single fight-day, discard.**

## Changes from current formula
**REMOVED (deleted entirely):**
- D-ribose (S2 20 g, S5 6 g, F5 3 g) — no avian skeletal-muscle evidence; highest Maillard/osmotic load (single highest-leverage cut)
- Korean corn syrup + maltodextrin (S2) — reducing-sugar Maillard stack
- Beta-alanine (S3 4 g) — chronic-only, avian muscle already carnosine/anserine-rich
- Rhodiola (caps/S1) — Tier-3, no avian basis
- All five wet liquids S2/S3/S4/S5/F5 — collapsed to dry; eliminates K-sorbate-at-pH-6 BLOCK, B12/P5P photolysis, NAC thiol oxidation, Maillard, microbial, cold-chain simultaneously
- K-sorbate (all SKUs) — only ~6% active at pH 6; redundant once dry
- Methyl-B12 in all liquids; Sodium ascorbate in reducing-sugar matrix (S2)
- Glycerin (no liquid left to humect)

**RE-DOSED / RELOCATED (liquid → dry):**
- NAC, L-glutamine, betaine, taurine, L-carnitine, B6, B12 → DRY PRELOAD (their correct chemical + chronic-mechanism home)
- Mg (S5 ~619 mg, Mg-malate/MgGP/MgCl2) → FEED ONLY (enteric ceiling); drink Mg = 0
- B6: consolidated from S3 100 mg + S4 B-complex + capsule P5P → single dry pyridoxine-HCl ~4 mg/bird/day (<15 mg/session by construction)
- Citrate alkali stack (heavy Na/K-citrate across all liquids) → trace 0.3 g/L Na-citrate buffer only; DEB +6.8 → +3.06 mEq/L

**SALT-FORM CHANGES:**
- Citrulline-malate → **free L-citrulline** (drops malate alkali load)
- K-citrate → **KCl**; bulk Na-citrate → **NaCl** (restores Cl, near-neutral Cl-balanced DEB)
- Methyl-B12 → **cyanocobalamin** (dry shelf stability)
- P5P → **pyridoxine-HCl** (dry shelf stability)
- Undefined Korean corn syrup → defined **dextrose monohydrate** (SGLT1 substrate, dose control)

**ADDED:**
- Dry ORS electrolyte backbone (NaCl/KCl) + dextrose; DMG moved into drink; trehalose as dry glassy-matrix stabilizer + heat-stress adjunct

## Cautions (written notes, not blockers)
## Welfare / Safety / Producibility Cautions (written notes — non-blocking)

**Welfare (scope = the bird's own recovery/hydration; dual-use, welfare-positive):**
- The SGLT1 hypotonic ORS + chronic antioxidant/osmolyte preload are welfare-positive: better rehydration, heat-stress and oxidative protection, ascites/pulmonary-hypertension mitigation (NAC, DMG, carnitine, betaine).
- Main residual welfare risk is microbial safety of any WET product — the dry/make-fresh design specifically removes it.

**Make-fresh discipline (the one operational risk that replaces the chemistry BLOCK):**
- The fight-day drink MUST be reconstituted fresh and discarded same-day. If a handler stores reconstituted sugar/citrulline solution warm for hours it can grow microbes. Mitigation: single-serve sticks, on-label "discard after session," acidified to pH ~3.8–4.2.

**Dosing caps (all met by construction):**
- B6 is dry-only single source → 0 mg/session in drink, ~4 mg/bird/day chronic — well under <15 mg/session. If any legacy B6-containing liquid is re-introduced by a handler, the cap can be breached; legacy liquids are DELETED and the label states single source.
- Mg is feed-only → 0 mg/session in drink. If feed Mg climbs toward 0.3–0.5% the enteric/laxative (wet-litter) ceiling applies — monitor droppings on scale-up.
- Repeated DAILY (vs occasional event) use shifts Na-load and B6-accumulation risk; the acute-safe conclusion is for OCCASIONAL event use.

**Timing / producibility:**
- Preload needs ~7–10 d lead time and assumes feed intake; a bird off-feed pre-event gets no creatine/betaine loading (timing risk, not chemistry).
- Hygroscopic actives (betaine, DMG/Na-DMG, trehalose dihydrate, Mg-glycerophosphate) and oxidation-prone NAC require foil + desiccant packaging; add NAC last under low humidity; geometric 2-step dilution for the B6/B12 micro-premix for uniformity.
- Trehalose dihydrate can cake/crystallize and shifts blend moisture — keep desiccated. (C. difficile dietary-trehalose virulence signal, PMID 29310122, is of UNKNOWN avian relevance — non-blocking monitoring note.)
- Cyanocobalamin substituted for methyl-B12 is a deliberate stability trade (more shelf-stable, adequate for poultry status repletion); confirm the methyl form is not specifically required.
- If a WET bottled SKU is ever forced against advice: pH ≤5.0–5.2 + a validated challenge-tested preservative system (NOT sorbate alone), never NAC/B12/P5P in it. Dry is strictly superior on producibility, shelf-life, and safety.

## Blocking gaps
- Chicken jejunal SGLT1 -> rehydration is a mechanistic INFERENCE: Shibata 2023 (PMID 37321034) endpoint is glucose-absorption ontogeny, not rehydration; SGLT1 also attenuates with market age (caveat). The hypotonic glucose+Na ORS is still the right vehicle regardless.
- No avian study demonstrates ACUTE single-session ergogenic benefit for DMG, citrulline, or the drink as a whole — drink actives are precautionary/physiologically-reasoned, not acute-efficacy-proven in birds. DMG/NAC/betaine/carnitine evidence is CHRONIC (preload) only.
- Exact 25C pharmacopeial water-solubility monographs are UNKNOWN for several actives (DMG ~50 mg/mL is an estimate; Mg-malate quantitative figure unverified, rests on conservative 50 mg/mL floor; methyl-B12 mg/mL uses cyanocobalamin 12.5 mg/mL analog). None change a GO/BLOCK verdict given large headroom.
- CORRECTED (codex 2026-06-16): DMG PMIDs 42184641 & 23571331 are REAL poultry papers (verify-lane false positive overturned). Real DMG set also valid: 20662970, 20666863, 21929832, 32370175. Citrulline>arginine PMID 28179487 IS mouse (DOI 10.3945/jn.116.240382) - cite chicken DOI 10.1186/s40104-022-00817-w as load-bearing. DEB rests on PMID 12619809 + WPSJ review (Moura PMID 39399982 unconfirmed).
- Field water intake under fight-stress is unverified: a hypotonic ORS only rehydrates if the bird drinks. Do NOT over-concentrate to compensate (would breach <300 mOsm).
