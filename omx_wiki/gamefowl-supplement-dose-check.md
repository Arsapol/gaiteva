# Gamefowl Supplement Dose Check

Category: reference  
Tags: gamefowl, dosing, safety, dilution, electrolyte-balance

## Protocol That Resolved The Gating Question

Bird: approximately `2.5 kg`  
Session: `6 rounds`  
Per-round concentrate:

- S2: `0.5 ml`
- S3: `1-2 ml`
- S4: `0.5 ml`
- S5: `0.5 ml`
- F5: `0.5 ml`

Worst-case total concentrate with S3 at `2 ml/round`:

- Per round: `4 ml concentrate`
- Per session: `24 ml concentrate`
- Dilution: `1 part concentrate : 3 parts water`
- Final drink volume: about `96 ml`
- Final osmolality: about `200 mOsm/kg`

This resolves the earlier mistake of judging bottle totals as if they were single-bird boluses.

Interpretation: modest acute alkalinizing load, with low chloride contribution.

## Safety Sanity Check

The measured fight-day protocol supports an **acute safety** conclusion for occasional use:

- Hypertonic osmolality concern is resolved by 1:3 dilution.
- Magnesium is far below a plausible laxative concern at this delivered dose.
- B6 alarm from human EFSA framing is dissolved at measured dose, but hidden B6 from S4 B-complex and capsules should still be counted.
- Citrulline is the ingredient that clearly lands at meaningful scale.
- Betaine dose is in a plausible effective range.
- D-ribose remains the clearest low-evidence cost/stability issue.

## Remaining Watch Items

- Count B6 from S4 B-complex and any capsule P5P if co-administered.
- Do not directly compare session `mEq` to diet `mEq/kg feed` without converting against daily feed/water exposure.
- Repeated daily long-term use may be a different risk profile than occasional session use.
- Field outcome is useful but observational; controlled comparison would be needed to prove performance or recovery effects.

## One-Line Final Verdict

Measured fight-day dosing shifts this to **acute-safe-on-the-numbers for occasional use**, with strongest rationale in DEB/alkali plus citrulline/betaine/NAC-DMG support, while ribose remains the clearest low-evidence cost/stability waste.
