# Substance: MCT oil (medium-chain triglycerides)

## 1. Identity
Medium-chain triglycerides (MCTs) are triglycerides enriched in C6-C12 fatty acids, commonly caprylic/capric triglycerides from coconut/palm-kernel fractions. In the inspected workbook `MCT oil 40% Oral Solution.xlsx`, the oil phase is 40 mL/100 mL in the first sheet and 30 mL/100 mL in the 30% sheet.

## 2. Three-rule findings
- **R1 solubility:** MCT oil is not water-soluble; the governing constraint is emulsion design, not aqueous molecular solubility. At 30-40% oil phase it requires a robust O/W emulsifier system and homogenization. Practical recommendation from the 2026-06-16 workbook analysis: pilot 20-25% first, cap at 30% unless syringeability, 7-day separation, and heat/cool testing pass.
- **R2 stability:** Triglyceride oil is generally chemically stable compared with labile vitamins/thiols, but must be protected from oxidation, rancidity, light/heat, and microbial risk in the aqueous phase. The aqueous phase/preservative system determines product shelf stability.
- **R3 compatibility:** No Maillard conflict (no reducing carbonyl or amine). Main conflicts are physical: phase separation, creaming, high viscosity with gums, and reduced water volume for dissolved salts/sugars. MCT does not require the carnitine shuttle as strongly as long-chain fats; therefore the workbook claim that L-carnitine directly pulls MCT into mitochondria is an overclaim. L-carnitine can still be a chronic poultry fat-metabolism/cardiopulmonary adjunct, especially under stress, but is not an acute MCT transporter.

## 3. Avian/poultry evidence
Poultry literature supports medium-chain fatty acids/glycerides mainly as dietary gut-health/fat-metabolism additives rather than acute fight-day bolus energy. Recent broiler studies/reviews report effects on lipid metabolism, gut health, and performance for dietary MCFA/MG inclusion. Direct evidence for a high-percentage MCT oral emulsion in gamefowl acute performance remains **UNKNOWN**.

## 4. Verdict for our use
**ADD-conditional / FLAG**: MCT is plausible as an energy-dense oil phase, but the current 40% formula is physically aggressive. For a liquid oral solution/emulsion, start at **20-25 mL MCT oil per 100 mL**, with **30 mL/100 mL as an upper pilot cap** only after emulsion and tolerance testing. Do not rely on L-carnitine as a direct acute MCT transporter.
