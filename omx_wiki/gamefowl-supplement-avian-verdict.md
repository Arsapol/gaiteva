# Gamefowl Supplement Avian Verdict

Category: reference  
Tags: gamefowl, poultry-nutrition, supplements, electrolyte-balance, avian-physiology

## Final Shared Verdict

Measured fight-day dosing shifts this formula set to **acute-safe-on-the-numbers for occasional use**, with the strongest poultry-science rationale in:

- DEB / alkali management from sodium and potassium citrate
- citrulline as an arginine-sparing / nitric-oxide axis ingredient
- betaine as an osmolyte and heat-stress support
- NAC and DMG as oxidative / heat-stress support
- creatine only as chronic preload, not acute rescue

The clearest low-evidence ingredient is **D-ribose**: no strong peer-reviewed avian skeletal-muscle performance basis, with cost, osmolality, and ribose-browning/stability concerns.

## Important Framing Shift

Do not judge this formula primarily with human supplement limits or WADA-style human sport rules.

Use avian / poultry nutrition science:

- Poultry values are usually expressed per kg complete feed, not per human daily dose.
- Birds do not sweat; thermoregulation is driven heavily by panting, respiratory alkalosis risk, heat load, water intake, and acid-base balance.
- Gamefowl burst performance is closer to repeated high-intensity avian stress physiology than human endurance sport.
- Safety must be judged by **delivered mg/kg bodyweight/session** and **mEq/session**, not bottle totals.

## Poultry Reference Anchors

- NRC 1994 poultry baseline: broiler diets are commonly anchored around sodium, potassium, chloride, magnesium, pyridoxine, and arginine requirements in complete-feed units.
- Dietary Electrolyte Balance: `DEB = Na + K - Cl`, usually expressed as `mEq/kg diet`.
- Poultry DEB literature commonly tests about `110-370 mEq/kg`; practical target is context-dependent, but a rough working range of `150-300 mEq/kg-equivalent` is more defensible than a single universal value.
- Under heat stress, higher DEB can support water intake and homeostasis, but excessive alkalinizing load can become counterproductive if daily/repeated.

## Ingredient Verdicts

| Ingredient / Axis | Avian-grounded verdict |
|---|---|
| DEB / citrates | Strongest rational lever. Quantify Na/K/Cl and net alkalinizing load. |
| Magnesium | Not a major risk at measured fight-day dose; concern is enteric/laxative load if delivered dose rises. |
| Sodium / potassium | Relevant and justified, but should be managed as DEB rather than generic electrolytes. |
| Vitamin B6 | Human EFSA 12 mg/day is the wrong frame. Poultry requirement is mg/kg feed; measured session dose is not auto-toxic. Still count hidden B6 from B-complex and capsules. |
| Creatine | Avian evidence supports chronic preload / muscle energy status. Weak as acute sip. Guanidinoacetic acid may be a better poultry creatine-system form. |
| Betaine | Avian-justified osmolyte / heat-stress support. |
| L-carnitine | Poultry evidence exists, but acute recovery claim is weaker than betaine / DEB / arginine. |
| Taurine | Birds synthesize taurine; not essential like cats. Conditional support only. |
| D-ribose | Weakest evidence and likely the main cost/stability waste. |
| Beta-alanine | Chronic-only; co-dependent on histidine; avian muscle is already carnosine-rich. |
| DMG | Avian-justified for heat-stress / gut / oxidative context, not a proven acute ergogenic. |
| NAC | Avian-justified for oxidative / heat-stress support at modest doses. |
| Arginine / citrulline | More important in birds than human framing suggests. Birds lack a functional urea cycle; arginine is strictly essential. Citrulline at gram scale is meaningful. |
| Rhodiola / adaptogens | Weak avian basis; possible anti-hypoxia / stress angle, not strong ergogenic evidence. |

## Gamefowl Physiology Notes

Gamefowl muscle use is dominated by fast-glycolytic burst work with repeated stress bouts. Likely limiting factors across repeated 20-minute rounds include:

- phosphocreatine and ATP turnover
- glycogen depletion
- lactate / H+ handling
- heat load and panting
- acid-base disturbance
- dehydration / water intake
- oxidative and inflammatory stress after exertion

Highest-confidence support levers:

1. DEB / alkali and water management
2. chronic creatine or GAA preload
3. betaine
4. arginine / citrulline at meaningful scale
5. NAC / DMG / magnesium as support, not primary drivers
