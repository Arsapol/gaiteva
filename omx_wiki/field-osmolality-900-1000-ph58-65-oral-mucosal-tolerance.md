# Field osmolality tolerance: 900–1000 mOsm/L at pH 5.8–6.5

Tags: osmolality, oral-mucosal-tolerance, field-evidence, formula-safety
Date: 2026-06-17

## Field finding

User-reported field testing: approximately 20 cock/gamefowl samples exposed by sublingual/oral route to formula samples measured around **900–1000 mOsm/L** with **pH ~5.8–6.5**. Reported outcome: **no observed oral tissue damage, oral issue, or harm** in the tested birds.

## Project classification update

For the same class of formula and same route/conditions, classify **900–1000 mOsm/L at pH 5.8–6.5** as:

- **SAFE / PASS for acute oral-mucosal tolerance**, when volume/contact time/excipients are comparable to the field-tested samples.
- **Still separate from hydration/GI safety**: high-osmolality swallowed fluids can remain a GI/hydration caution, especially in dehydrated/sick animals or when used as a hydration drink.
- **Not universal across formulas**: unsafe excipients, extreme acidity/alkalinity, irritant solvents/surfactants, or much larger volumes can override this classification.

## Evidence boundary

This is practical field evidence, not controlled histopathology. It supports acute visible-tissue tolerance in the tested condition; it does not by itself prove absence of microscopic lesions, repeated-use safety, or safety for dehydrated/diseased birds.

## External reference anchors

- WHO/UNICEF ORS target remains much lower (245 mOsm/L) for oral rehydration solutions, showing that hydration-drink targets are not the same as short-contact mucosal tolerance.
- Veterinary oral-electrolyte literature flags extremely hypertonic products (>700 mOsm/L) mainly for GI/hydration effects such as diarrhea risk, not necessarily direct oral tissue burn.
- Avian fluid-therapy references place isotonic fluid targets near ~300–320 mOsm/L, again a hydration/fluid-replacement target rather than a sublingual concentrate tissue-tolerance threshold.
