# Substance: Beta-Alanine

# Substance Dossier: Beta-Alanine

## 1. Identity
β-alanine (3-aminopropanoic acid). CAS 107-95-9. Rate-limiting precursor of muscle carnosine (β-alanyl-L-histidine), an intracellular H+ buffer.

## 2. Three-rule findings
- **R1 solubility:** very soluble in water (~550+ g/L) — OK for solution.
- **R2 stability:** stable amino acid in solution; reducing-sugar Maillard partner (avoid in dextrose drinks).
- **R3 conflicts:** Maillard with reducing sugars (dextrose/ribose) over storage → keep out of sugar drinks; co-needs histidine for carnosine synthesis.

## 3. Avian/poultry evidence
Raises broiler breast-muscle carnosine dose-dependently; CHRONIC (~6 wk); best with L-histidine co-supplementation (Qi 2021 PMC8388462, VERIFIED). β-alanine is the PRIMARY limiting precursor; histidine becomes co-limiting only when β-alanine is supplemented in excess (verifier nuance). Avian pectoralis already carnosine/anserine-rich → smaller marginal upside than mammals.

## 4. Verdict
ADD-conditional — PRELOAD only (chronic carnosine buffer loading). ZERO acute fight-day value. In-scope (recovery buffer, not aggression agent).
