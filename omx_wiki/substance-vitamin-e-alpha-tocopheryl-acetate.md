# Substance: Vitamin E (alpha-tocopheryl acetate)

# Substance: Vitamin E (alpha-tocopheryl acetate / tocopherol)

## 1. Identity
Vitamin E feed/supplement forms include all-rac-alpha-tocopheryl acetate / DL-alpha-tocopheryl acetate (CAS 7695-91-2) and natural alpha-tocopherol forms. Candidate use in Gaiteva V3: **alpha-tocopheryl acetate oily feed/food grade in MCT phase, 200 mg/100 mL** (≈10 mg per 5 mL dose).

## 2. Three-rule findings
- **R1 solubility:** Vitamin E acetate is fat/oil-soluble and water-insoluble. It belongs in the MCT oil phase, not the water phase. At 200 mg/20 mL oil phase = 10 mg/mL oil-phase loading, expected physical fit is good, but exact supplier miscibility must be confirmed.
- **R2 stability:** Acetate form is the stable commercial feed/liquid form. BASF technical information for Vitamin E-Acetate Oily Feed states it is soluble in fats/oils, insoluble in water, should be stored dry/protected from light/not above 30°C, and unopened original packaging is stable at least 24 months. In the finished emulsion, 6–12 month potency still requires assay because water/emulsion/oxygen/light can differ from neat packaging.
- **R3 conflicts:** No Maillard conflict. It can protect lipid phase from oxidative rancidity, but is itself light/oxygen sensitive. No known precipitation with electrolytes because it stays in oil droplets. Use amber/opaque bottle, low headspace, and avoid trace-metal contamination.

## 3. Avian/poultry evidence
Vitamin E has direct poultry heat-stress support. Reviews and trials report dietary vitamin E at roughly **100–250+ mg/kg feed** improving heat-stress performance, antioxidant status, immune parameters, mortality, and/or oxidative meat/liver markers in broilers. It is not an acute CNS focus agent; its strongest evidence is chronic antioxidant/tissue protection.

## 4. Verdict for our use
**ADD-strong/conditional** for the MCT emulsion oil phase. It raises the antioxidant/stability score and protects the lipid system better than wet ascorbate. Do not use it as the only antioxidant claim if acute anti-stress is the target; pair with make-fresh dry ascorbate/taurine if higher antioxidant score is needed.

## Update 2026-06-16 — verifier correction on oil-protection claim

Independent verifier `Cicero` returned **CORRECTED**: vitamin E acetate remains plausible and no avian dose red flag was found at ~10 mg per 5 mL dose, but the in-bottle lipid-protection claim must be softened.

- Keep **alpha-tocopheryl acetate** primarily as a stable vitamin E source / bird antioxidant support.
- Do **not** claim it strongly protects the MCT oil phase by itself. For oil-phase antioxidant protection, prefer a dedicated food/feed antioxidant system such as mixed tocopherols/free alpha-tocopherol/ascorbyl palmitate, plus oxygen/light control; those excipient choices need their own safety/regulatory check before final formula lock.
- BASF raw-material stability (24+ months protected unopened packaging) does **not** prove finished emulsion stability. Finished bottle needs real-time/accelerated testing: phase separation, droplet size, pH, preservative challenge, peroxide/anisidine values, and HPLC assay for CoQ10/vitamin E.

Verifier verdict: **Vitamin E acetate = ADD-conditional; 6 months plausible with validation; 12 months unconfirmed without data.**
